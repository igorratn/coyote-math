For complex $z \in \mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\}$ and $n \in \mathbb{N}$, consider the Bessel function of the first kind $J_n(nz)$ defined by the integral:

$$J_n(nz) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{|t|=1} e^{\frac{nz}{2}(t - 1/t)} t^{-n-1} dt$$

where the contour is the unit circle traversed counter-clockwise. Let $z = i\beta$, where $\beta > 1$ is a real constant. Let $S_n = |J_n(in\beta)|$.

Claim: The sequence $S_n$ remains bounded as $n \to \infty$.

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and provide a rigorous proof.

The claim is False.

Proof.

The Bessel function of the first kind is related to the modified Bessel function $I_n$. For real $x$, the identity $J_n(ix) = i^n I_n(x)$ holds. Setting $x = n\beta$:

$$S_n = |J_n(in\beta)| = |i^n I_n(n\beta)| = I_n(n\beta)$$

The modified Bessel function $I_n(x)$ has the series expansion

$$I_n(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k!(n+k)!}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^{n+2k},$$

which consists entirely of nonnegative terms for $x>0$. In particular, keeping only the leading term gives the estimate

$$I_n(x)\ge \frac{1}{n!}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^n.$$

Substituting $x=n\beta$ yields

$$S_n=I_n(n\beta)\ge \frac{1}{n!}\left(\frac{n\beta}{2}\right)^n.$$

Applying Stirlingâ€™s formula $n!\sim \sqrt{2\pi n}(n/e)^n$, we find

$$\frac{1}{n!}\left(\frac{n\beta}{2}\right)^n \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi n}}\left(\frac{e\beta}{2}\right)^n.$$

Since $\beta>1$, the base $(e\beta)/2$ exceeds $1$, and the right-hand side grows without bound as $n\to\infty$. Consequently $S_n\to\infty$, and the sequence is not bounded.
