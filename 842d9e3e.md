The spherical harmonics $Y_{\ell m}(\theta,\phi)$ satisfy three-term recurrence relations. One such relation connects harmonics of consecutive degree $\ell$ at fixed magnetic quantum number $m$:

$$\cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m}(\theta,\phi) = A_{\ell m} Y_{\ell+1,m}(\theta,\phi) + B_{\ell m} Y_{\ell-1,m}(\theta,\phi)$$

where $A_{\ell m}$ and $B_{\ell m}$ are constants depending on $\ell$ and $m$ but not on $\theta$ or $\phi$.

The spherical harmonics are orthonormal on the sphere:

$$\int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} Y_{\ell_1 m_1}(\theta,\phi) \overline{Y_{\ell_2 m_2}(\theta,\phi)} \sin\theta \, d\theta \, d\phi = \delta_{\ell_1 \ell_2} \delta_{m_1 m_2}$$

Consider a modified recurrence relation where we replace $\cos\theta$ by $\cos^2\theta$:

$$\cos^2\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m}(\theta,\phi) = C_{\ell m} Y_{\ell+2,m}(\theta,\phi) + D_{\ell m} Y_{\ell,m}(\theta,\phi) + E_{\ell m} Y_{\ell-2,m}(\theta,\phi)$$

Claim: For fixed $m$ and $\ell \geq 2$, the modified recurrence can be expressed using only three terms (degree $\ell+2$, $\ell$, and $\ell-2$) with constant coefficients $C_{\ell m}$, $D_{\ell m}$, and $E_{\ell m}$ that depend only on $\ell$ and $m$.

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.

The claim is True.

The key insight is to apply the standard recurrence relation twice. Start with

$$\cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m} = A_{\ell m} Y_{\ell+1,m} + B_{\ell m} Y_{\ell-1,m}$$

Multiply both sides by $\cos\theta$ again:

$$\cos^2\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m} = A_{\ell m} \cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell+1,m} + B_{\ell m} \cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell-1,m}$$

Now apply the recurrence relation to each term on the right. For the first term,

$$\cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell+1,m} = A_{\ell+1,m} Y_{\ell+2,m} + B_{\ell+1,m} Y_{\ell,m}$$

For the second term,

$$\cos\theta \cdot Y_{\ell-1,m} = A_{\ell-1,m} Y_{\ell,m} + B_{\ell-1,m} Y_{\ell-2,m}$$

Substituting back,

$$\cos^2\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m} = A_{\ell m}(A_{\ell+1,m} Y_{\ell+2,m} + B_{\ell+1,m} Y_{\ell,m}) + B_{\ell m}(A_{\ell-1,m} Y_{\ell,m} + B_{\ell-1,m} Y_{\ell-2,m})$$

Collecting terms by degree,

$$\cos^2\theta \cdot Y_{\ell m} = (A_{\ell m} A_{\ell+1,m}) Y_{\ell+2,m} + (A_{\ell m} B_{\ell+1,m} + B_{\ell m} A_{\ell-1,m}) Y_{\ell,m} + (B_{\ell m} B_{\ell-1,m}) Y_{\ell-2,m}$$

Therefore, the coefficients are

$$C_{\ell m} = A_{\ell m} A_{\ell+1,m}$$
$$D_{\ell m} = A_{\ell m} B_{\ell+1,m} + B_{\ell m} A_{\ell-1,m}$$
$$E_{\ell m} = B_{\ell m} B_{\ell-1,m}$$

Since $A_{\ell m}$ and $B_{\ell m}$ depend only on $\ell$ and $m$ (not on $\theta$ or $\phi$), the coefficients $C_{\ell m}$, $D_{\ell m}$, and $E_{\ell m}$ also depend only on $\ell$ and $m$.

Crucially, only three terms appear: $Y_{\ell+2,m}$, $Y_{\ell,m}$, and $Y_{\ell-2,m}$. The intermediate terms $Y_{\ell+1,m}$ and $Y_{\ell-1,m}$ cancel when collecting coefficients, confirming the claim.
