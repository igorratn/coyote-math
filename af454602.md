Let $H_\nu^{(1)}(z)$ denote the Hankel function of the first kind of order $\nu > 0$, which satisfies the Bessel differential equation

$$z^2 u'' + z u' + (z^2 - \nu^2) u = 0$$

and is analytic in $\mathbb{C} \setminus (-\infty, 0]$ with branch cut along the negative real axis. It satisfies the outgoing-wave asymptotic

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(z) \sim \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi z}} \exp\left(i\left(z - \frac{\nu\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right)$$

as $|z| \to \infty$ in the sector $-\pi + \delta < \arg z < 2\pi - \delta$ for any fixed $\delta > 0$, where $\sqrt{z}$ uses the principal branch ($\arg\sqrt{z} \in (-\pi/2, \pi/2)$).

For $r > 0$ large and $\theta \in (-\pi, \pi)$, set $z = r e^{i\theta}$. Define the normalized function

$$w(r,\theta) = H_\nu^{(1)}(r e^{i\theta}) \sqrt{\frac{\pi r e^{i\theta}}{2}} \exp\left( -i \left( r e^{i\theta} - \frac{\nu\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4} \right) \right).$$

**Claim:** There exists a constant $C = C(\nu) > 0$ independent of $r$ and $\theta$ such that for all sufficiently large $r > 0$ and all $\theta \in (-\pi, \pi)$ with $H_\nu^{(1)}(r e^{i\theta}) \neq 0$,

$$|w(r,\theta) - 1| \le \frac{C}{r}.$$

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.

Claim False in general. The bound fails for all $\nu$ with $\cos(\pi\nu) \neq 0$.

Fix $\epsilon > 0$. Nikiforov–Uvarov, Chapter III, §15, Eq.(5) (p. 209) states that for any fixed integer $n \ge 1$ and any fixed $\epsilon > 0$, uniformly on $|\arg z| \le \pi - \epsilon$ as $|z| \to \infty$,

$$H_\nu^{(1,2)}(z) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi z}} \exp\left(\pm i \left(z - \frac{\nu\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right) \left[ \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} C_k \left(\frac{\pm i}{z}\right)^k + O\left(\frac{1}{z^n}\right) \right]$$

with $C_0 = 1$. Taking $n=1$ gives the leading form:

$$H_\nu^{(2)}(z) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi z}} \exp\left(-i \left(z - \frac{\nu\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right) (1 + O(1/z))$$

uniformly on such subsectors.

The uniform estimate in the claim must hold as $\theta \to -\pi$, so behavior near the branch cut must be examined. Nikiforov–Uvarov, Chapter III, §15, Eq.(11) gives:

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(z) = \frac{J_{-\nu}(z) - e^{-i\pi\nu} J_\nu(z)}{i \sin(\pi\nu)}, \qquad H_\nu^{(2)}(z) = \frac{e^{i\pi\nu} J_\nu(z) - J_{-\nu}(z)}{i \sin(\pi\nu)}$$

Under analytic continuation $z \mapsto z e^{-i\pi}$, one has $J_\nu(z e^{-i\pi}) = e^{-i\pi\nu} J_\nu(z)$ and $J_{-\nu}(z e^{-i\pi}) = e^{i\pi\nu} J_{-\nu}(z)$ (from the phase rule $(z e^{-i\pi})^{\pm\nu} = e^{\mp i\pi\nu} z^{\pm\nu}$ in the defining power series of $J_{\pm\nu}$, i.e., Eq.(12)). Substituting into Eq.(11) yields the exact continuation identity:

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(z e^{-i\pi}) = -e^{i\pi\nu} H_\nu^{(2)}(z)$$

Now set $\theta_r = -\pi + 1/r$ and $z_r = r e^{i\theta_r}$, and define $\zeta_r = z_r e^{i\pi} = r e^{i/r}$. Then $\arg \zeta_r \to 0$, so the asymptotic above applies to $H_\nu^{(2)}(\zeta_r)$. Since $z_r = \zeta_r e^{-i\pi} = -\zeta_r$, the continuation identity gives:

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(z_r) = H_\nu^{(1)}(\zeta_r e^{-i\pi}) = -e^{i\pi\nu} H_\nu^{(2)}(\zeta_r)$$

Insert this into the definition of $w$ at $z = z_r$ and substitute the leading asymptotic for $H_\nu^{(2)}(\zeta_r)$:

$$w(r, \theta_r) = -e^{i\pi\nu} H_\nu^{(2)}(\zeta_r) \sqrt{\frac{\pi z_r}{2}} \exp\left(-i \left(z_r - \frac{\nu\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right)$$

$$= -e^{i\pi\nu} \sqrt{\frac{z_r}{\zeta_r}} \exp\left(-i \left((z_r + \zeta_r) - \nu\pi - \frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right) (1 + O(1/r))$$

Using $z_r = -\zeta_r$ gives $z_r + \zeta_r = 0$ and $z_r/\zeta_r = e^{-i\pi}$, so on the principal branch $\sqrt{z_r/\zeta_r} = \sqrt{e^{-i\pi}} = -i$. Therefore:

$$w(r, \theta_r) \to -e^{i\pi\nu} (-i) \exp\left(i\pi\nu + \frac{i\pi}{2}\right) = -e^{i2\pi\nu}$$

Hence:

$$|w(r, \theta_r) - 1| \to |-e^{i2\pi\nu} - 1| = 2|\cos(\pi\nu)|$$

If $\cos(\pi\nu) \neq 0$, this limit is a positive constant, contradicting any bound $|w(r, \theta) - 1| \le C/r$ with $C$ independent of $\theta$ on $(-\pi, \pi)$. Thus, the claim is false.

Reference. A. F. Nikiforov and V. B. Uvarov, Special Functions of Mathematical Physics, Birkhäuser, 1988, Chapter III, §15, Eq.(5) (p. 209) and Eq.(11)–(12) (p. 211).
