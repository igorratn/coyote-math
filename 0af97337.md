Let $r, p > 0$, let $\theta$ be the angle between the vectors of lengths $r$ and $p$, and let $R = \sqrt{r^2 + p^2 - 2rp \cos \theta}$.

For $r < p$, Graf’s addition theorem states:

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(kR) e^{i\nu\psi} = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} J_n(kr) H_{\nu+n}^{(1)}(kp) e^{in\theta}, \qquad r < p$$

where $\psi$ is the angle at the vertex corresponding to the side $p$ in the triangle with sides $r, p, R$.

Consider the formally symmetric series obtained by interchanging $r$ and $p$:

$$H_\nu^{(1)}(kR) e^{i\nu\psi'} = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} J_n(kp) H_{\nu+n}^{(1)}(kr) e^{in\theta}$$

Claim: This symmetric formula for $p < r$ follows from the original $r < p$ formula by analytically continuing the series representation across the boundary $r = p$, that is, by using the same analytic continuation argument applied to the original Graf expansion.

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.


Claim is false.

Away from the singular locus $R=0$, the underlying function $H_\nu^{(1)}(kR)e^{i\nu\psi}$ admits analytic continuation across the region $r=p$. However, the specific Graf series representation does not.

The original series $\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} J_n(kr) H_{\nu+n}^{(1)}(kp) e^{in\theta}$ converges only for $r < p$. Asymptotic analysis shows the general term scales as $(r/p)^{|n|}$, so the series diverges for $r > p$.

Analytic continuation of a function does not preserve a particular series expansion outside its domain of convergence. In the region $p < r$, the same function admits a different expansion,

$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} J_n(kp) H_{\nu+n}^{(1)}(kr) e^{in\theta}.$$

This symmetric formula is a separate local representation, not a term-by-term continuation of the first series. The claim is false because it incorrectly identifies continuation of the function with continuation of a divergent series.
