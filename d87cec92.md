The Kelvin functions of order zero are defined for real $x > 0$ by

$$J_0\!\bigl(x\,e^{i\pi/4}\bigr) = \operatorname{ber}(x) + i\,\operatorname{bei}(x), \qquad K_0\!\bigl(x\,e^{i\pi/4}\bigr) = \operatorname{ker}(x) + i\,\operatorname{kei}(x),$$

where $J_0$ is the Bessel function of the first kind and $K_0$ is the modified Bessel function of the second kind, both of order zero. The four real-valued functions $\operatorname{ber}$, $\operatorname{bei}$, $\operatorname{ker}$, $\operatorname{kei}$ form a fundamental system of solutions to the fourth-order equation $\nabla^4 u = u$ in polar coordinates (equivalently, to $x^2 y'' + xy' - ix^2 y = 0$ when decomposed into real and imaginary parts).

Define the two individual Wronskians

$$W_1(x) = \operatorname{ber}(x)\,\operatorname{ker}'(x) - \operatorname{ber}'(x)\,\operatorname{ker}(x),$$
$$W_2(x) = \operatorname{bei}(x)\,\operatorname{kei}'(x) - \operatorname{bei}'(x)\,\operatorname{kei}(x).$$

Claim: For all $x > 0$,

$$W_1(x) - W_2(x) = \frac{1}{x}.$$

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.
