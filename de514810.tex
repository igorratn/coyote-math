In a Frenkel–Kontorova chain without external drive, the misfit field $\Theta(x,t)$ satisfies the damped sine–Gordon equation $\Theta_{tt}+\eta\Theta_t-c^2\Theta_{xx}+\sin\Theta=0$ for $x\in\mathbb R$, where $\eta>0$ and $c>0$. Assume zero topological winding, $\Theta(+\infty,t)=\Theta(-\infty,t)\in2\pi\mathbb Z$. The chain is prepared at rest with a uniform configuration $\Theta_t(x,0)=0$, $\Theta(x,0)=\pi+\varepsilon$, where $\varepsilon>0$ is arbitrarily small and spatially uniform. Determine whether $\lim_{t\to+\infty}\Theta(x,t)=0$ holds, or whether the field relaxes to a different asymptotic state.

Reduce to the uniform ODE: spatially uniform data keep $\Theta(x,t)=\vartheta(t)$, so $\vartheta_{tt}+\alpha\vartheta_t+\sin\vartheta=0$ with $\vartheta_t(0)=0$ and the given $\vartheta(0)$ in $(\pi,3\pi)$. The energy $E(t)=\tfrac12\vartheta_t^2+1-\cos\vartheta$ satisfies $\dot E=-\alpha\vartheta_t^2\le0$, hence $E$ is nonincreasing and the motion must settle at a stationary point where $\sin\vartheta=0$, with stable equilibria at $2\pi k$. Since $E(0)=1-\cos\vartheta(0)<2=1-\cos\pi=1-\cos3\pi$, the trajectory cannot surmount the barrier at $\vartheta=\pi$ or $\vartheta=3\pi$, so it remains in the interval $(\pi,3\pi)$ and converges to the unique stable minimum there, namely $\vartheta=2\pi$. Therefore $\lim_{t\to+\infty}\Theta(x,t)=2\pi$.
