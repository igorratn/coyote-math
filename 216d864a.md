Let $Y_{\ell m}(\theta, \phi)$ denote the standard spherical harmonics on the unit sphere $\mathbb{S}^2$ with normalization $$Y_{\ell m}(\theta, \phi) = \sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}\frac{(\ell-m)!}{(\ell+m)!}} P_{\ell}^m(\cos\theta) e^{im\phi}$$ where $P_{\ell}^m$ are the associated Legendre functions and $(\theta, \phi) \in [0,\pi] \times [0,2\pi)$. For integers $\ell \geq 1$ and $k \geq 0$, define the weighted moment integral $$M_{\ell,k} = \int_0^\pi \sin^{2k+1}\theta \sum_{m=-\ell}^{\ell} |Y_{\ell m}(\theta, 0)|^2 \, d\theta$$ Claim For all integers $\ell \geq 1$ and all $k \geq 0$, the weighted moment satisfies $$M_{\ell,k} = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi} \cdot \frac{2^{2k+1} k! \, (k+1)!}{(2k+2)!}$$ Determine with rigorous proof whether the claim is True or False.

Conclusion: False

At $\phi = 0$, the spherical harmonic becomes

$$Y_{\ell m}(\theta, 0) = \sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}\frac{(\ell-m)!}{(\ell+m)!}} P_{\ell}^m(\cos\theta)$$


By the spherical harmonic addition theorem,

$$\sum_{m=-\ell}^{\ell}Y_{\ell m}(\theta_1,\phi_1)\overline{Y_{\ell m}(\theta_2,\phi_2)}=\frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}P_\ell(\cos\gamma),$$

where $\gamma$ is the angle between the two directions $(\theta_1,\phi_1)$ and $(\theta_2,\phi_2)$. In the present expression one has $|Y_{\ell m}(\theta,0)|^2=Y_{\ell m}(\theta,0)\overline{Y_{\ell m}(\theta,0)}$, so $(\theta_1,\phi_1)=(\theta_2,\phi_2)=(\theta,0)$, hence $\gamma=0$, $\cos\gamma=1$, and $P_\ell(1)=1$, giving

$$\sum_{m=-\ell}^{\ell}|Y_{\ell m}(\theta,0)|^2=\frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}.$$

For $k=0$, this gives:

$$M_{\ell, 0} = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi} \int_0^\pi \sin\theta \, d\theta = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi} \cdot 2 = \frac{2\ell+1}{2\pi}$$

The claimed formula gives for $k=0$:

$$M_{\ell, 0} = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi} \cdot \frac{2^1 \cdot 0! \cdot 1!}{2!} = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi} \cdot 1 = \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}$$

Since $\frac{2\ell+1}{2\pi} \neq \frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}$, the claim is false.
