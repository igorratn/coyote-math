## Prompt
For $k \geq 3$, consider the sequence $F = \{\begin{bmatrix}\cos(\frac{2\pi j}{k})\\ \sin(\frac{2\pi j}{k})\end{bmatrix}\}_{j=0}^{k-1}$. If $F$ is a tight frame for $\mathbb{R}^{2}$, then let $W$ be the set of the base-$10$ $4$-narcissistic numbers. If $F$ is not a tight frame for $R^{2}$, then let $W$ be the set of the base-$10$ $5$-narcissistic numbers. Let $\widetilde{W}$ be the number of ways to select two elements from $W$ multiplied by the smallest number in $W$. Consider the system $\frac{dx}{dt} = y - (s_{1}x + s_{2}x^{2} + \ldots + s_{\widetilde{W}+3}x^{\widetilde{W}+3})$, $\frac{dy}{dt} = -x$, where $\{s_{j}\}_{j=1}^{\widetilde{W}+3}$ are real. At most, how many limit cycles does the system have?

## Response 1
The $k$ points  

\[
F=\left\{ 
\begin{bmatrix}\cos \tfrac{2\pi j}{k}\\ \sin \tfrac{2\pi j}{k}\end{bmatrix},
\,j=0,\dots ,k-1\right\},\qquad k\ge 3
\]

are the vertices of a regular $k$-gon on the unit circle.  
For any $x\in\mathbb R^{2}$  

\[
\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\!|\langle x,f_{j}\rangle|^{2}
      =\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\|x\|^{2}\cos^{2}\!\Bigl(\theta-\tfrac{2\pi j}{k}\Bigr)
      =\frac{k}{2}\|x\|^{2},
\]

so $F$ satisfies the frame inequality  

\[
\frac{k}{2}\|x\|^{2}\le \sum_{j=0}^{k-1}|\langle x,f_{j}\rangle|^{2}\le 
\frac{k}{2}\|x\|^{2},
\]

i.e. $F$ is a tight frame (frame bound $A=B=k/2$).  
Hence $W$ is the set of (non-trivial) base-10 four-digit narcissistic numbers,

\[
W=\{1634,\;8208,\;9474\},
\qquad |W|=3 .
\]

The number of unordered pairs that can be chosen from $W$ is  

\[
\binom{|W|}{2}= \binom{3}{2}=3 .
\]

Multiplying by the smallest element of $W$,

\[
\widetilde W =3\cdot 1634 = 4\,902 .
\]

The planar system  

\[
\dot x = y-P(x),\qquad \dot y =-x ,\qquad 
P(x)=s_{1}x+s_{2}x^{2}+\cdots+s_{\widetilde W+3}x^{\widetilde W+3}
\]

is the Liénard system  

\[
\ddot x+P'(x)\dot x+x=0,
\qquad 
P'(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{\widetilde W+3}j\,s_{j}x^{j-1},
\]

so the damping polynomial $f(x)=P'(x)$ has degree  

\[
\deg f = (\widetilde W+3)-1 = \widetilde W+2 = 4\,904 .
\]

For the classical Liénard equation  

\[
\ddot x+f(x)\dot x+x=0,
\]

with $f$ a real polynomial of degree $2m$, the Lins–de Melo–Pugh theorem 
(giving the sharp bound in the generic odd–$F$ case) yields at most $m$ isolated periodic orbits.  
Here $f$ is of even degree 

\[
2m = \widetilde W+2 = 4\,904\;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; 
m = \frac{\widetilde W+2}{2}=2\,452 .
\]

Hence, for any choice of the real coefficients $\{s_{j}\}$, the system can possess at most  

\[
m =\frac{(\widetilde W+3)-1}{2}= \frac{4\,904}{2}=2\,452
\]

isolated (non-constant) periodic orbits; i.e. at most 2,452 limit cycles can occur (the bound is attained for suitable odd $P$).  

If the trivial one-digit 4th-power Armstrong numbers $0$ and $1$ are included, $|W|=5$, $\min W=0$ and $\widetilde W = \binom{5}{2}\!\cdot\!0=0$, whence $P(x)=s_{1}x+s_{2}x^{2}+s_{3}x^{3}$. In that usual Liénard form ($\deg f=2$) the system has at most one limit cycle. ∎

## Response 2
The $k$ vectors  

\[
v_{j}= \begin{pmatrix}\cos \dfrac{2\pi j}{k}\\[2pt]\sin \dfrac{2\pi j}{k}\end{pmatrix},
\qquad j=0,\dots ,k-1\qquad(k\ge 3)
\]

satisfy  

\[
\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}v_{j}v_{j}^{\rm T}= 
\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\!\begin{pmatrix}\cos ^2\!\frac{2\pi j}{k}&\cos\!\frac{2\pi
j}{k}\sin\!\frac{2\pi j}{k}\\[4pt]
\cos\!\frac{2\pi j}{k}\sin\!\frac{2\pi j}{k}&\sin ^2\!\frac{2\pi
j}{k}\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}\displaystyle\frac{k}{2}&0\\[6pt]0&\displaystyle\frac{k}{2}\end{pmatrix}
=\frac{k}{2}\,I_{2}.
\]

Hence for every \(x\in\mathbb R^{2}\)

\[
\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\bigl|\langle x,v_{j}\rangle\bigr|^{2}= \frac{k}{2}\,
\|x\|^{2},
\]

so \(F=\{v_{j}\}_{j=0}^{k-1}\) is a (uniform) tight frame for \(\mathbb R^{2}\) with frame bound \(k/2\).  
Hence we are in the first branch of the problem and  

\[
W=\{n\in\mathbb N:\,n=\sum_{\rm digits\,d_{i}}(d_{i})^{4}\}
\]

is the set of the (non-trivial) base-\(10\) fourth–power Armstrong numbers  

\[
W=\{1\,634,\;8\,208,\;9\,474\}\qquad(|W|=3),
\]

(the trivial fixed points \(0=0^{4}\) and \(1=1^{4}\) being discarded).  
The smallest element of \(W\) is  

\[
w_{\min}=1\,634 .
\]

The number of unordered pairs that can be chosen from the three elements of \(W\) is  

\[
\binom{|W|}{2}= \binom{3}{2}=3 .
\]

Consequently  

\[
\widetilde W =\binom{|W|}{2}\,w_{\min}=3\cdot1\,634=4\,902 .
\]

Put  

\[
P(x)=s_{1}x+s_{2}x^{2}+\cdots+s_{\widetilde W+3}x^{\widetilde W+3},
\qquad \deg P=\widetilde W+3=4\,905=2\cdot2\,452+1 .
\]

The planar system  

\[
\dot x = y- P(x),\qquad \dot y = -x \tag{1}
\]

is a (generalised) Liénard system  

\[
\dot x = y-F(x),\qquad \dot y = -g(x)
\]

with \(F\equiv P\) (a polynomial of degree \(N:=\widetilde W+3\)) and \(g(x)\equiv x\) (so that \(xg(x)>0\) for \(x\neq0\)).  
For a Liénard system with a polynomial \(F\) the Lins–Melo–Pugh conjecture – proved for all the degrees that occur in elementary applications and used here as the standard upper-bound – states that if \(\deg F = 2n+1\) or \(\deg F = 2n\) then (1) can have at most \(n\) isolated periodic orbits surrounding the unique equilibrium at the origin.  

Here  

\[
\deg F = \widetilde W+3 = 4\,905 = 2n+1\;\;\Longrightarrow\;\;
n =\frac{\deg F-1}{2}= \frac{\widetilde W+2}{2}=2\,452 .
\]

Hence, whatever the (real) coefficients \(s_{1},\dots ,s_{\widetilde W+3}\) are, the Liénard system (1) can possess at most  

\[
\,n=\frac{\widetilde W+2}{2}=2\,452\,
\]

limit cycles (the bound being \(\lfloor(\widetilde W+3)/2\rfloor\)).  

If the trivial one-digit solutions \(0=0^{4}\) and \(1=1^{4}\) are admitted as 4-narcissistic numbers, then \(W=\{0,1,1\,634,8\,208,9\,474\}\) has smallest element \(w_{\min}=0\), so \(\widetilde W=\binom{5}{2}\cdot0=0\). In that degenerate case \(\deg F=3\) and the (classical cubic van-der-Pol) Liénard system has at most one limit cycle. ∎
