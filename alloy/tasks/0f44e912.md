## Prompt
Show that the ground state of a Cooper pair box with $E_{J} \ll E_{c}$ and $n_{g}=\frac{1}{2}$ is $\frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$.

## Response 1
The island (charge) basis $|\,n\rangle$, $n=0,\pm1,\dots$ , is the eigenbasis of the excess Cooper-pair number $\hat n$, $\hat n|n\rangle =n|n\rangle$. With the total island capacitance $C_{\Sigma}$ the charging energy, $E_{C}= (2e)^{2}/2C_{\Sigma}=2e^{2}/C_{\Sigma}$ (so that $E_{C}({\hat n}-n_{g})^{2}$ is the electrostatic energy; if $E_{C}=e^{2}/2C_{\Sigma}$ the charging term is $4E_{C}({\hat n}-n_{g})^{2}$, the algebra is unchanged) and the dimensionless gate charge $n_{g}=C_{g}V_{g}/2e$ the Hamiltonian of the box is $$\hat H =E_{C}\,({\hat n}-n_{g})^{2}-E_{J}\cos\hat\varphi ,\qquad [\hat\varphi ,{\hat n}]=i$$ and $$e^{\pm i\hat\varphi}= \sum_{n}|n\pm1\rangle\langle n|, \qquad \cos\hat\varphi=\tfrac12\big(e^{i\hat\varphi}+e^{-i\hat\varphi}\big) =\frac12\sum_{n}\Big(|n\rangle\langle n+1|+|n+1\rangle\langle n|\Big).$$ Hence in the charge basis $$\hat H = \sum_{n}E_{C}(n-n_{g})^{2}\,|n\rangle\langle n| -\frac{E_{J}}{2}\sum_{n}\Big(|n\rangle\langle n+1| +|n+1\rangle\langle n|\Big).$$ Acting on $|\psi\rangle=\sum_{n}c_{n}|n\rangle$, $\hat H|\psi\rangle=E|\psi\rangle$ gives the tight-binding recursion $$E_{C}(n-n_{g})^{2}c_{n}-\frac{E_{J}}{2}\,(c_{n+1}+c_{n-1})=Ec_{n}\;, \qquad\sum_{n}|c_{n}|^{2}=1.$$

For $E_{J}=0$ the eigenstates are the pure charge states $|\,n\rangle$ with $E_{n}^{(0)}=E_{C}(n-n_{g})^{2}$. At the gate charge $n_{g}= \tfrac12$, $E_{0}^{(0)}=E_{1}^{(0)}=E_{C}\Big(\tfrac12\Big)^{2}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}$, whereas $E_{-1}^{(0)}=E_{2}^{(0)}=E_{C}\Big(\tfrac32\Big)^{2}= \frac{9E_{C}}{4} = E_{0}^{(0)}+2E_{C}$, etc. Hence the two lowest unperturbed levels, $|0\rangle$ (no excess pair) and $|1\rangle$ (one excess Cooper pair), are exactly degenerate and are separated from all other charge states by $\sim 2E_{C}\gg E_{J}$. For $0<E_{J}\ll E_{C}$ the Josephson term can be treated in first-order degenerate perturbation theory in the manifold ${\cal H}_{0}={\rm span}\{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\}$; admixture of the remote states $|n|\ge2$ is $$|c_{\pm1}|\sim\frac{E_{J}/2}{E_{\pm1}^{(0)}-E_{0}^{(0)}}\sim \frac{E_{J}}{4E_{C}}\ll1$$ and can be neglected to ${\cal O}(E_{J}/E_{C})$.

Projecting onto ${\cal H}_{0}$ we retain only $\langle0|\hat H|0\rangle=\langle1|\hat H|1\rangle=E_{C}/4$ and $\langle0|\hat H|1\rangle = \langle1|\hat H|0\rangle = -\frac{E_{J}}{2}$. In the ordered basis $(|0\rangle,|1\rangle)^{T}$, $$H_{\rm qb}= \begin{pmatrix} E_{C}/4 & -E_{J}/2\\ -E_{J}/2 & E_{C}/4 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{E_{C}}{4}\,{\bf 1}-\frac{E_{J}}{2}\,\sigma_{x},$$ which commutes at $n_{g}=1/2$ with the charge-inversion $\hat P|n\rangle=|1-n\rangle$, so the eigenstates are the even and odd combinations of $|0\rangle$ and $|1\rangle$. Diagonalising, $$E_{\pm}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}\mp\frac{E_{J}}{2}.$$ Hence $$|+\rangle=\frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt2},\qquad E_{+}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}-\frac{E_{J}}{2},$$ $$|-\rangle=\frac{|0\rangle-|1\rangle}{\sqrt2},\qquad E_{-}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}+\frac{E_{J}}{2}.$$ Because $E_{J}>0$, $|+\rangle$ is the lowest-energy eigenstate of the projected Hamiltonian.

The exact ground-state wavefunction in the phase representation, $\psi_{0}(\varphi)\propto \exp(i n_{g}\varphi)\,{\rm ce}_{0}(\varphi/2,q)$ with $q=E_{J}/2E_{C}$, is for $q\ll1$ and $n_{g}=1/2$ $\psi_{0}(\varphi)\simeq\sqrt{2/\pi}\,\cos(\varphi/2)$, giving $c_{0}=c_{1}=1/\sqrt2+{\cal O}(q)$, $c_{n\neq0,1}={\cal O}(q)$. Thus, to leading order in $E_{J}/E_{C}$ and exactly at the charge-degeneracy (sweet) point $n_{g}=1/2$, the Cooper-pair box reduces to the charge qubit and its ground state is the symmetric superposition $$|g\rangle = |+\rangle = \frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt2} + {\cal O}\!\left(\frac{E_{J}}{E_{C}}\right).$$ The orthogonal combination $|e\rangle=(|0\rangle-|1\rangle)/\sqrt2$ is the first excited charge-qubit state, split from the ground state by the Josephson energy $E_{-}-E_{+}=E_{J}$. At $n_{g}=1/2$ the qubit is first-order insensitive to charge noise, $\partial E_{\pm}/\partial n_{g}=0$. ∎

## Response 2
The island–plus–junction system is described in the charge basis $|n\rangle$, $n\in\mathbb Z$ being the number of excess Cooper pairs on the island ($[\,\hat\varphi,\hat n\,]=i$), by $$\hat H = E_{C}\sum_{n}(n-n_{g})^{2}\,|n\rangle\langle n| -\frac{E_{J}}{2}\sum_{n}\bigl(|n\rangle \langle n+1|+ |n+1\rangle \langle n|\bigr) \equiv \hat H_{C}+\hat H_{J}, \qquad n_{g}=C_{g}V_{g}/2e,$$ with $E_{C}=(2e)^{2}/2C_{\Sigma}$ the Cooper–pair charging energy and $\hat H_{J}=-E_{J}\cos\hat\varphi$. For $E_{J}=0$ the eigenstates are the charge states $|n\rangle$ with electrostatic energies $E_{n}^{(0)} =E_{C}(n-n_{g})^{2}$.

At the gate charge $n_{g}= \tfrac12$ the two lowest electrostatic levels become degenerate, $E_{0}^{(0)}=E_{C}(\tfrac12)^{2}= E_{C}/4=E_{1}^{(0)}$, whereas the next charge states are separated by $\sim E_{C}$. For $E_{J}\ll E_{C}$, only the almost-degenerate doublet $\{|0\rangle ,|1\rangle\}$ is populated and $H_{J}$ can be treated in the degenerate subspace. Projecting (1) on $\mathcal H_{0}={\rm span}\{|0\rangle ,|1\rangle\}$, $$\hat H_{0}=P_{0}\hat HP_{0} =\frac{E_{C}}{4}\bigl(|0\rangle\langle0|+|1\rangle\langle1|\bigr) -\frac{E_{J}}{2}\bigl(|0\rangle\langle1|+|1\rangle\langle0|\bigr) +O\!\left(\frac{E_{J}}{E_{C}}\right).$$ In the $\{|0\rangle ,|1\rangle\}$ basis $$H_{0}\doteq \begin{pmatrix} E_{C}/4 & -E_{J}/2\\ -E_{J}/2 & E_{C}/4 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{E_{C}}{4}\,{\bf 1}-\frac{E_{J}}{2}\sigma_{x}.$$ Diagonalising, $E_{\pm}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}\mp\frac{E_{J}}{2}$. For $E_{-}=E_{C}/4-E_{J}/2$ the equations give $a_{-}=b_{-}$, hence $$|g\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\bigl(|0\rangle+|1\rangle\bigr),\qquad E_{g}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}-\frac{E_{J}}{2}+O\!\left(\frac{E_{J}^{2}}{E_{C}}\right),$$ while for $E_{+}=E_{C}/4+E_{J}/2$ one obtains $a_{+}=-b_{+}$, $$|e\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\bigl(|0\rangle-|1\rangle\bigr),\qquad E_{e}= \frac{E_{C}}{4}+\frac{E_{J}}{2}+O\!\left(\frac{E_{J}^{2}}{E_{C}}\right).$$ Because $E_{J}>0$, $E_{-}<E_{+}$; $|g\rangle$ is the non-degenerate ground state of the truncated Hamiltonian.

If the coupling to $|n|=2,3,\dots$ is retained, ordinary second-order perturbation theory gives the admixture $$|\Psi_{g}\rangle = \frac{|0\rangle+|1\rangle}{\sqrt2} +\frac{E_{J}}{8E_{C}}\,\frac{|{-1}\rangle+|2\rangle}{\sqrt2} +O\!\left[\Bigl(\tfrac{E_{J}}{E_{C}}\Bigr)^{2}\right],$$ so that for $E_{J}/E_{C}\ll1$ the ground state remains, to an excellent approximation, $|g\rangle\simeq (|0\rangle+|1\rangle)/\sqrt2$. At $E_{J}=0$ the two charge states are exactly degenerate and any $\alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle$ is a ground state; an infinitesimal $E_{J}$ selects the even (bonding) combination, which has $\langle\hat n\rangle_{g}= \tfrac12$ and $\partial E_{g}/\partial n_{g}=0$ at $n_{g}=1/2$, the charge-noise sweet spot. The odd combination $|e\rangle=(|0\rangle-|1\rangle)/\sqrt2$ is the first excited qubit level, split from the ground state by $E_{e}-E_{g}=E_{J}+O(E_{J}^{2}/E_{C})$. ∎
