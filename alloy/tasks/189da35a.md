## Prompt
Prove that for any real numbers $a, b, c$:
$$a^{2}+(2-\sqrt{2})b^{2}+c^{2}\ge \sqrt{2}(ab-bc+ca)$$

## Response 1
$$a^{2}+(2-\sqrt2 )b^{2}+c^{2}\ge \sqrt2\,(ab-bc+ca),\qquad a,b,c\in \mathbb R$$

Put $t=\sqrt2\;(t^{2}=2)$ and bring everything to the left

$$F(a,b,c)=a^{2}+(2-t)b^{2}+c^{2}-t ab+tbc-tca\ge 0 .$$

### 1. Complete the square in $a$

$$\begin{aligned}F &=a^{2}-ta(b+c)+(2-t)b^{2}+c^{2}+tbc \\ &=\Bigl(a-\frac t2 (b+c)\Bigr)^{2} -\frac{t^{2}}4(b+c)^{2}+(2-t)b^{2}+c^{2}+tbc .\end{aligned}$$

Since $t^{2}=2$,

$$-\frac{t^{2}}4(b+c)^{2}= -\frac12\bigl(b^{2}+2bc+c^{2}\bigr).$$

Hence

$$F=\Bigl(a-\frac t2 (b+c)\Bigr)^{2} +\underbrace{\Bigl(\frac32-t\Bigr)b^{2}+(t-1)bc+\frac12c^{2}}_{=:G(b,c)} .$$

### 2. The remaining quadratic in $b,c$

$G(b,c)=A b^{2}+B bc+C c^{2}$ with $A=\frac32-t>0$, $B=t-1$, $C=\frac12$.

Its discriminant is

$$\Delta =B^{2}-4AC=(t-1)^{2}-2\Bigl(\frac32-t\Bigr) =t^{2}-2t+1-3+2t =t^{2}-2=0 .$$

Thus $G$ is a perfect square:

$$G=A\Bigl(b+\frac{B}{2A}c\Bigr)^{2}, \qquad \frac{B}{2A}= \frac{t-1}{2(\tfrac32-t)} =\frac{t-1}{3-2t}=t+1 .$$

Consequently

$$G(b,c)=\Bigl(\tfrac32-t\Bigr)\bigl(b+(t+1)c\bigr)^{2} =\frac{(t-1)^{2}}{2}\,\bigl(b+(t+1)c\bigr)^{2}\ge 0 .$$

### 3. Sum of two squares

Therefore

$$F= \Bigl(a-\frac{\sqrt2}{2}(b+c)\Bigr)^{2} +\frac{(\sqrt2-1)^{2}}{2}\,\bigl(b+(\sqrt2+1)c\bigr)^{2}\ge 0,$$

which is the required inequality.

### Equality

Equality holds iff both squares vanish, i.e.

$$a=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}(b+c),\qquad b=-(\sqrt2+1)c \;\Longleftrightarrow\; (a,b,c)=\lambda\bigl(\sqrt2-1,\;1,\;1-\sqrt2\bigr),\;\lambda\in\mathbb R .$$

Hence for all real $a,b,c$

$$a^{2}+(2-\sqrt2)b^{2}+c^{2}\ge\sqrt2\,(ab-bc+ca),$$

with equality only for $(a,b,c)$ proportional to $(\sqrt2-1,1,1-\sqrt2)$. âˆŽ

## Response 2
$$Q:=a^{2}+(2-\sqrt2)b^{2}+c^{2}-\sqrt2(ab-bc+ca) =a^{2}- \sqrt2ab-\sqrt2ca+c^{2}+(2-\sqrt2)b^{2}+\sqrt2bc .$$

Write $\sqrt2=s$. Completing the square in $a$,

$$\begin{aligned}Q&=\Bigl(a-\frac{s}{2}(b+c)\Bigr)^{2} -\frac{s^{2}}{4}(b+c)^{2}+c^{2}+(2-s)b^{2}+sbc \\ &=\Bigl(a-\frac{s}{2}(b+c)\Bigr)^{2} -\frac12(b+c)^{2}+c^{2}+(2-s)b^{2}+sbc .\end{aligned}$$

The part that depends only on $(b,c)$ is

$$-\frac12(b+c)^{2}+c^{2}+(2-s)b^{2}+sbc = \frac{(s-1)^{2}}{2}b^{2}+(s-1)bc+\frac12c^{2}.$$

Because $(s-1)^{2}=3-2s$,

$$\frac{(s-1)^{2}}{2}b^{2}+(s-1)bc+\frac12c^{2} =\frac12\bigl((s-1)b+c\bigr)^{2}.$$

Hence

$$Q=\Bigl(a-\frac{\sqrt2}{2}(b+c)\Bigr)^{2} +\frac12\bigl((\sqrt2-1)b+c\bigr)^{2}\ge 0 .$$

Therefore

$$a^{2}+(2-\sqrt2)b^{2}+c^{2}\ge\sqrt2(ab-bc+ca)\qquad(\forall a,b,c\in\mathbb R).$$

Equality holds when both squares vanish:
$$a=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}(b+c),\qquad c=(1-\sqrt2)b,$$
i.e. for $(a,b,c)=k\bigl(\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}(2-\sqrt2),1,1-\sqrt2\bigr),\ k\in\mathbb R$. âˆŽ
