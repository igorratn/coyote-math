# Evaluation Criteria (from Handshake task page)

## 1. Correctness
Usually the most important factor. The response should be factually accurate and logically sound, especially in expert domains where mistakes are critical.

- **Math:** valid proof, sound steps, correct result
- **Medicine:** accurate diagnosis, appropriate treatment, contraindications addressed
- **Chemistry:** correct mechanism, products, and stereochemistry
- **Law:** sound reasoning and correct application of legal principles

A correct response should beat a polished but incorrect one.

## 2. Completeness
The response should fully address the prompt, not just part of it.

- Covers all parts of a multi-part question
- Includes key caveats or edge cases
- Acknowledges uncertainty where relevant

## 3. Clarity and Communication
The response should be easy to follow and appropriately structured.

- Explains reasoning clearly, ideally step by step
- Uses a logical structure
- Stays concise without unnecessary filler

## 4. Domain Appropriateness
The response should match the norms and language of the field.

- Uses standard terminology and notation
- Follows domain conventions
- Sounds appropriate for the subject matter
