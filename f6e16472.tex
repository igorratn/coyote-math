Let ${p_n(x)}_{n=0}^\infty$ be orthonormal polynomials with respect to a positive weight $w(x)$ on $[a,b]$, satisfying the recurrence

$$xp_n(x)=a_{n+1}p_{n+1}(x)+b_n p_n(x)+a_n p_{n-1}(x)\quad(a_{n+1}>0).$$

For fixed $x_0\in(a,b)$ and $N\ge1$, consider the linear polynomial

$$\pi_t(x)=p_{N+1}(x)-tp_N(x).$$

Determine (with proof) whether

$$\inf_{t\in\mathbb R}\frac{\|\pi_t\|_w^2}{\pi_t(x_0)^2} =\frac{1}{a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr]} =\inf_{\substack{Q\in\mathbb P_N\\Q(x_0)\ne0}}\frac{\|Q\|_w^2}{Q(x_0)^2}.$$

We show that the claimed equality is false in general.

Let $\{p_n(x)\}_{n=0}^\infty$ be orthonormal on $[a,b]$ with respect to $w(x)$ and satisfy

$$x p_n(x)=a_{n+1}p_{n+1}(x)+b_n p_n(x)+a_n p_{n-1}(x)\quad(a_{n+1}>0).$$

Fix $x_0\in(a,b)$ and $N\ge1$, and set

$$\pi_t(x)=p_{N+1}(x)-t p_N(x),\qquad t\in\mathbb R.$$

Define

$$A=p_N(x_0),\quad B=p_{N+1}(x_0).$$

Because $\{p_n\}$ is orthonormal,

$$\|\pi_t\|_w^2=\|p_{N+1}\|_w^2-2t\langle p_{N+1},p_N\rangle_w+t^2\|p_N\|_w^2=1+t^2.$$

Also

$$\pi_t(x_0)=B-tA.$$

Hence the ratio is

$$F(t)=\frac{\|\pi_t\|_w^2}{\pi_t(x_0)^2}=\frac{1+t^2}{(B-tA)^2}.$$

To find $\inf_{t\in\mathbb R}F(t)$, differentiate:

$$F'(t)=\frac{2t(B-tA)^2-(1+t^2)2(B-tA)(-A)}{(B-tA)^4}.$$

The critical points satisfy $F'(t)=0$, i.e.,

$$t(B-tA)^2+(1+t^2)A(B-tA)=0.$$

If $B-tA\neq0$ (i.e., $t\neq B/A$), we can divide by $B-tA$ and get

$$t(B-tA)+(1+t^2)A=0,$$

that is

$$tB-t^2A+A+At^2=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad tB+A=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad t_*=-\frac{A}{B}$$

(provided $B\neq0$; if $B=0$ the computation below still gives the correct infimum by taking limits).

At $t=t_*$ we have

$$1+t_*^2=1+\frac{A^2}{B^2}=\frac{A^2+B^2}{B^2},$$

$$(B-t_*A)^2=\left(B+\frac{A^2}{B}\right)^2=\left(\frac{A^2+B^2}{B}\right)^2=\frac{(A^2+B^2)^2}{B^2},$$

so

$$F(t_*)=\frac{(A^2+B^2)/B^2}{(A^2+B^2)^2/B^2}=\frac{1}{A^2+B^2}.$$

As $t\to\pm\infty$,

$$F(t)\sim\frac{t^2}{t^2A^2}=\frac{1}{A^2},$$

so the infimum of $F(t)$ over $t\in\mathbb R$ is

$$\inf_{t\in\mathbb R}\frac{\|\pi_t\|_w^2}{\pi_t(x_0)^2}=\frac{1}{A^2+B^2}=\frac{1}{p_N(x_0)^2+p_{N+1}(x_0)^2}.$$

On the other hand, by the standard theory of orthogonal polynomials, the Christoffelâ€“Darboux kernel

$$K_N(x,y)=\sum_{j=0}^N p_j(x)p_j(y)$$

satisfies the CD identity and its diagonal limit

$$K_N(x_0,x_0)=a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr],$$

and the Christoffel function

$$\lambda_N(x_0)=\inf_{\substack{Q\in\mathbb P_N\\Q(x_0)\ne0}}\frac{\|Q\|_w^2}{Q(x_0)^2}$$

equals

$$\lambda_N(x_0)=\frac{1}{K_N(x_0,x_0)}=\frac{1}{a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr]}.$$

Thus in general

$$\inf_{t\in\mathbb R}\frac{\|\pi_t\|_w^2}{\pi_t(x_0)^2}=\frac{1}{p_N(x_0)^2+p_{N+1}(x_0)^2},$$

while

$$\inf_{\substack{Q\in\mathbb P_N\\Q(x_0)\ne0}}\frac{\|Q\|_w^2}{Q(x_0)^2} =\frac{1}{a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr]}.$$

These two expressions are not equal in general (for generic orthogonal systems $p_N(x_0)^2+p_{N+1}(x_0)^2\ne a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr]$), so the claimed equality

$$\inf_{t\in\mathbb R}\frac{\|\pi_t\|_w^2}{\pi_t(x_0)^2} =\frac{1}{a_{N+1}\bigl[p_{N+1}'(x_0)p_N(x_0)-p_{N+1}(x_0)p_N'(x_0)\bigr]} =\inf_{\substack{Q\in\mathbb P_N\\Q(x_0)\ne0}}\frac{\|Q\|_w^2}{Q(x_0)^2}$$

is false in general.
