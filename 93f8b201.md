Let $Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \phi)$ be the standard spherical harmonics on $\mathbb{S}^{2}$, normalized in $L^{2}(\mathbb{S}^{2})$, with integers $\ell \ge 0$ and $|m| \le \ell$.

The spherical harmonics are defined by

$$Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \phi) = \sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{4\pi}\frac{(\ell-m)!}{(\ell+m)!}} P_{\ell}^{m}(\cos\theta) e^{im\phi}$$

where $P_{\ell}^{m}$ denotes the associated Legendre function and $(\theta, \phi) \in [0, \pi] \times [0, 2\pi)$.

Claim: 
Fix $m \in \mathbb{Z}$. There exist constants $C > 0$ and $\delta \in (0, 1)$, independent of $\ell$, such that for all integers $\ell \ge |m|$,

$$\sup_{\theta \in [0, \pi]} \Bigl| (\sin\theta)^{|m|+\delta} Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \phi) \Bigr| \le C$$

Determine, with rigorous justification, whether the statement is true or false.


Answer: True. 

For $m=0$ one may take any $\delta \in [1/2, 1)$. For $|m| \ge 1$ any $\delta \in (0, 1)$ works.

Proof.

Since $|e^{im\phi}| = 1$ and $|Y_{\ell}^{-m}| = |Y_{\ell}^{m}|$, it suffices to consider $m \ge 0$. Put $x = \cos\theta$ and $n = \ell - m$. In A. F. Nikiforov and V. B. Uvarov, Special Functions of Mathematical Physics, Birkhäuser, 1988, Chapter II, §10, the spherical harmonics are separated as

$$Y_{\ell m}(\theta, \phi) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{im\phi} \Theta_{\ell m}(\cos\theta), \quad \text{[p. 80, Eq. (10)]}$$

and the bounded angular factor is represented by the Jacobi form

$$\Theta_{\ell m}(x) = C_{\ell m} (1 - x^2)^{m/2} P_{\ell-m}^{(m,m)}(x), \quad \text{[p. 78, Eq. (6)]}$$

Choosing $A_{\ell m} = 1$ gives the explicit normalization constant

$$C_{\ell m} = \frac{1}{2^{m} m!} \sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{2} (\ell-m)! (\ell+m)!}, \quad \text{[p. 79]}.$$

Therefore,

$$|Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \phi)| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} |\Theta_{\ell m}(x)| = \frac{|C_{\ell m}|}{\sqrt{2\pi}} (1 - x^2)^{m/2} |P_{n}^{(m,m)}(x)|.$$

Define $B_{\ell m} := |C_{\ell m}| / \sqrt{2\pi} \ge 0$. For fixed $m$, Stirling’s formula applied to the explicit expression for $C_{\ell m}$ shows $B_{\ell m} \le K_m\sqrt{\ell}$ for some $K_m$ depending only on $m$. Consequently, $B_{\ell m} / \sqrt{n}$ is uniformly bounded for all $\ell \ge m+1$.

Next we use the Jacobi envelope estimate from Nikiforov and Uvarov, Chapter II, §7:

$$(1-x)^{\alpha/2+1/4} (1+x)^{\beta/2+1/4} |P_{n}^{(\alpha, \beta)}(x)| \le \frac{A}{\sqrt{n}}, \quad \text{[p. 53, Eq. (19)]}$$

With $\alpha = \beta = m \ge 0$ this yields $|P_{n}^{(m,m)}(x)| \le A n^{-1/2} (1 - x^2)^{-m/2-1/4}$. Substituting this into the representation of $Y_{\ell}^{m}$ gives, for $n \ge 1$:

$$(\sin\theta)^{m+\delta} |Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \phi)| \le A \frac{B_{\ell m}}{\sqrt{n}} (1 - x^2)^{m/2+\delta/2-1/4}.$$

If $m \ge 1$, then $m/2 + \delta/2 - 1/4 > 0$ for every $\delta \in (0, 1)$, hence $(1 - x^2)^{m/2+\delta/2-1/4} \le 1$ on $[-1, 1]$, and the expression is uniformly bounded. If $m=0$, boundedness holds exactly when $\delta \ge 1/2$; if $\delta < 1/2$, the term $(1 - x^2)^{\delta/2-1/4}$ diverges as $x \to \pm 1$.

The case $n=0$ (where $\ell=m$) corresponds to a fixed continuous function proportional to $(\sin\theta)^{2m+\delta}$, which is bounded on $[0, \pi]$. Replacing $m$ by $|m|$ completes the proof.
