Let $m$ be a fixed non-negative integer and $l \ge m+1$.

The angular functions for the spherical harmonics are

$$\Theta_{lm}(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2l+1}{2} \frac{(l-m)!}{(l+m)!}} P_l^m(x)$$

where $x = \cos\theta$ and $P_l^m(x) = (1-x^2)^{m/2} \frac{d^m}{dx^m} P_l(x)$ are the associated Legendre functions, with $P_l(x)$ the Legendre polynomials.

For $z \in \mathbb{C} \setminus [-1, 1]$, define the second kind function

$$Q_{lm}(z) = \int_{-1}^1 \frac{\Theta_{lm}(t)}{z - t} dt$$

Define

$$\Phi_l^m(z) = \Theta_{lm}(z) Q_{l-1,m}(z) - \Theta_{l-1,m}(z) Q_{lm}(z)$$

Claim: For every $l \ge m+1$, the function $\Phi_l^m(z)$ is independent of $z$ on $\mathbb{C} \setminus [-1, 1]$ and satisfies

$$\Phi_l^m(z) = \frac{2^{2m+1} l \cdot (l-1)!^2}{(l-m)(l+m) \cdot (l+m-1)! \cdot (l-m-1)!}$$

Determine whether this claim is true or false, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.


The claim is false.

For fixed $m$, the functions $\Theta_{\ell m}$ form an orthonormal system in $L^{2}([-1,1])$ with unit weight, using the normalization of Nikiforov–Uvarov (_Special Functions of Mathematical Physics_, Ch. II, §10). From the standard spherical-harmonic recurrence one obtains

$x\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(x) = a_{\ell-1}\Theta_{\ell m}(x)+a_{\ell-2}\Theta_{\ell-2,m}(x),$ 

$\qquad a_{\ell-1}=\sqrt{\frac{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)}{(2\ell-1)(2\ell+1)}}.$

Define the Cauchy transforms

$$Q_{\ell m}(z)=\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{\Theta_{\ell m}(t)}{z-t}dt, \qquad z\in\mathbb C\setminus[-1,1].$$

Dividing the $\Theta$ recurrence by $z-t$ and integrating over $[-1, 1]$ gives

$$\int_{-1}^{1} \frac{t\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(t)}{z-t}dt = a_{\ell-1} Q_{\ell m}(z) + a_{\ell-2} Q_{\ell-2,m}(z).$$

Using $\frac{t}{z-t} = \frac{z}{z-t} - 1$, the left side becomes $z Q_{\ell-1,m}(z) - \int_{-1}^{1} \Theta_{\ell-1,m}(t)dt$. The constant function 1 is proportional to the ground-state function $\Theta_{00}$. Since the family $\{\Theta_{\ell m}\}$ is orthonormal on $[-1,1]$, we have $\langle \Theta_{\ell-1,m},\Theta_{00}\rangle=0$ whenever $(\ell-1,m)\neq(0,0)$. Under the standing assumption $\ell\ge m+1$, this condition is always satisfied, and therefore

$$\int_{-1}^{1}\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(t)dt=0.$$

This leaves the recurrence for the Cauchy transforms:

$$zQ_{\ell-1,m}(z) = a_{\ell-1} Q_{\ell m}(z) + a_{\ell-2} Q_{\ell-2,m}(z).$$

Evaluating the $\Theta$-recurrence at $t=z$ gives

$$z\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(z)=a_{\ell-1}\Theta_{\ell m}(z)+a_{\ell-2}\Theta_{\ell-2,m}(z).$$

Multiplying the $Q$ recurrence by $\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(z)$ and the $\Theta$ recurrence by $Q_{\ell-1,m}(z)$ and subtracting yields the first-order recurrence for $\Phi_\ell^m(z)=\Theta_{\ell m}(z)Q_{\ell-1,m}(z)-\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(z)Q_{\ell m}(z)$:

$$a_{\ell-1}\Phi_\ell^m(z) = a_{\ell-2}\Phi_{\ell-1}^m(z).$$


If $\Phi_\ell^m(z)$ were independent of $z$, write $C_\ell = \Phi_\ell^m(z)$. The recurrence $a_{\ell-1}\Phi_\ell^m(z) = a_{\ell-2}\Phi_{\ell-1}^m(z)$ then forces

$$\frac{C_\ell}{C_{\ell-1}} = \frac{a_{\ell-2}}{a_{\ell-1}}$$

$$\frac{C_\ell}{C_{\ell-1}} = \sqrt{\frac{(\ell-m-1)(\ell+m-1)}{(2\ell-3)(2\ell-1)} \cdot \frac{(2\ell-1)(2\ell+1)}{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)}}$$

$$\frac{C_\ell}{C_{\ell-1}} = \sqrt{\frac{(\ell-m-1)(\ell+m-1)(2\ell+1)}{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)(2\ell-3)}}.$$

On the other hand, the claimed value is

$$C_\ell^{\text{claim}} = \frac{2^{2m+1}\,\ell\,(\ell-1)!^{2}}{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)\,(\ell+m-1)!\,(\ell-m-1)!}.$$

A direct computation of successive ratios gives

$$\frac{C_\ell^{\text{claim}}}{C_{\ell-1}^{\text{claim}}} = \frac{\ell(\ell-1)}{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)}.$$

These ratios disagree for $\ell \ge m+2$. Therefore the claimed factorial expression cannot be the $z$-independent value of $\Phi_\ell^m$.

This contradiction shows that the claim is false.
