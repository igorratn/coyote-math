Let $m$ be a fixed non-negative integer and $l \ge m+1$.

The angular functions for the spherical harmonics are

$$\Theta_{lm}(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2l+1}{2} \frac{(l-m)!}{(l+m)!}} P_l^m(x)$$

where $x = \cos\theta$ and $P_l^m(x) = (1-x^2)^{m/2} \frac{d^m}{dx^m} P_l(x)$ are the associated Legendre functions, with $P_l(x)$ the Legendre polynomials.

For $z \in \mathbb{C} \setminus [-1, 1]$, define the second kind function

$$Q_{lm}(z) = \int_{-1}^1 \frac{\Theta_{lm}(t)}{z - t} dt$$

Define

$$\Phi_l^m(z) = \Theta_{lm}(z) Q_{l-1,m}(z) - \Theta_{l-1,m}(z) Q_{lm}(z)$$

Claim: For every $l \ge m+1$, the function $\Phi_l^m(z)$ is independent of $z$ on $\mathbb{C} \setminus [-1, 1]$ and satisfies

$$\Phi_l^m(z) = \frac{2^{2m+1} l \cdot (l-1)!^2}{(l-m)(l+m) \cdot (l+m-1)! \cdot (l-m-1)!}$$

Determine whether this claim is true or false, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.


False.

For fixed $m$, the functions $\Theta_{\ell m}$ form an orthonormal system in $L^{2}([-1,1])$ (with weight 1) by construction. Hence they satisfy a three–term recurrence in $\ell$ of the form:

$$x\Theta_{\ell-1,m}(x) = a_{\ell-1}\Theta_{\ell m}(x) + a_{\ell-2}\Theta_{\ell-2,m}(x)$$

Inserting the standard $\ell$-recurrence for $P_{\ell}^{m}$:

$$(\ell-m)P_{\ell}^{m}(x) = (2\ell-1)xP_{\ell-1}^{m}(x) - (\ell+m-1)P_{\ell-2}^{m}(x)$$

Together with the explicit normalization $\Theta_{\ell m} = N_{\ell m}P_{\ell}^{m}$, this gives:

$$a_{\ell-1} = \sqrt{\frac{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)}{(2\ell-1)(2\ell+1)}}$$

Now define the Cauchy transforms $Q_{\ell m}(z) = \int_{-1}^{1}\frac{\Theta_{\ell m}(t)}{z-t}dt$.
Using the three–term recurrence for $\Theta_{\ell m}(t)$, dividing by $z-t$ and integrating over $[-1,1]$, one obtains the corresponding recurrence for $Q_{\ell m}(z)$ with an extra constant term, namely 1; subtracting the two recurrences shows that for orthonormal $\{\Theta_{\ell m}\}$:

$$\Phi_{\ell}^{m}(z) := \Theta_{\ell m}(z)Q_{\ell-1,m}(z) - \Theta_{\ell-1,m}(z)Q_{\ell m}(z)$$

is independent of $z \in \mathbb{C} \setminus [-1,1]$ and equals:

$$\Phi_{\ell}^{m} = \frac{1}{a_{\ell-1}} = \sqrt{\frac{(2\ell-1)(2\ell+1)}{(\ell-m)(\ell+m)}}$$

This constant does not match the claimed factorial expression (which has very different $\ell$- and $m$-dependence), so the claim as stated is false.
