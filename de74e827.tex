Let $j_1,j_2$ be fixed half-integers with $j_1,j_2\ge2$, and let $m$ be an integer with $|m|\le j_1+j_2-2$.
For each allowed total angular momentum $j_3$ satisfying $|j_1-j_2|\le j_3\le j_1+j_2$ and $m_3=m$, consider the Clebsch–Gordan coefficient
$ f(j_3)=\langle j_1,0;,j_2,m\mid j_3,m\rangle. $
Introduce the discrete variable
$ x=j_1+j_2-j_3, $
so that $x$ runs over all integers from $x_{\min}=|j_1-j_2|$ to $x_{\max}=j_1+j_2$.

Consider the explicit second-order three-term difference equation
$ A(x)f(x+1)+B(x)f(x)+C(x)f(x-1)=0, $
where
$ A(x)=\sqrt{(x+1)(x+2j_1+1)(x+m+1)(x+2j_2-m+1)}, $
$ C(x)=\sqrt{x(x+2j_1)(x+m)(x+2j_2-m)}, $
$ B(x)=-(A(x)+C(x)). $

Prove or disprove that, for every admissible $x$, the Clebsch–Gordan coefficient $f(x)$ satisfies
$ A(x)f(x+1)+B(x)f(x)+C(x)f(x-1)=0. $

For fixed half-integers $j_1,j_2\ge2$ and an integer $m$ with $|m|\le j_1+j_2-2$, the Clebsch–Gordan coefficients
$$f(j_3)=\langle j_1,0;j_2,m\mid j_3,m\rangle$$
satisfy the standard three–term recurrence in the total angular momentum $j_3$ (Varshalovich, Moskalev and Khersonskii, Quantum Theory of Angular Momentum, Eq.(8.3.1.8)).

Introduce the integer variable
$$x=j_1+j_2-j_3$$
and define $f(x)=f(j_1+j_2-x)$. For allowed $j_3$ one has $|j_1-j_2|\le j_3\le j_1+j_2$, so $x$ runs over the admissible integers
$$0\le x\le 2\min(j_1,j_2).$$

After substituting $j_3=j_1+j_2-x$ into the standard recurrence and clearing the denominators $4j_3^2(4j_3^2-1)$, the relation assumes the quadratic dual–Hahn form (Biedenharn and Louck, Angular Momentum in Quantum Physics, Chapter 9, Eqs.(9.131)–(9.133))
$$A(x)f(x+1)+B(x)f(x)+C(x)f(x-1)=0\quad(\lambda=0)$$
with
$$A(x)=(x+2j_1)(x+2j_1-2m),$$
$$C(x)=x(x-2m),$$
$$B(x)=-(x+2j_1)(x+2j_1-2m)-x(x-2m)+2(x+j_1)(x+j_1-m).$$

Thus the function $f(x)$ satisfies a second–order three–term difference equation in $x$ with the explicit coefficients $A(x),B(x),C(x)$ above, and this recurrence is exactly the dual Hahn polynomial recurrence associated with the stretched Clebsch–Gordan sector $m_1=0$.
