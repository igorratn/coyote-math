Let the Clebsch–Gordan coefficient $\langle1,0;2,0\mid2,0\rangle$ be given. Determine (with proof) whether this value corresponds to a root of the associated Hahn polynomial in the discrete variable $x=j_1-m_1$.

We take $j_1=1,\ m_1=0,\ j_2=2,\ m_2=0,\ j_3=2,\ m_3=0$.

Use the standard relation between Clebsch–Gordan coefficients and Wigner $3j$ symbols (Edmonds, _Angular Momentum in Quantum Mechanics_, Princeton University Press, 1957, Ch.4):

$$\langle j_1,m_1,j_2,m_2\mid j_3,m_3\rangle=(-1)^{j_1-j_2+m_3}\sqrt{2j_3+1}\begin{pmatrix}j_1&j_2&j_3\\m_1&m_2&-m_3\end{pmatrix}.$$

Thus

$$\langle1,0;2,0\mid2,0\rangle\propto\begin{pmatrix}1&2&2\\0&0&0\end{pmatrix}.$$
For integer $j_1,j_2,j_3$ one has the parity rule for Wigner $3j$ symbols with all $m_i=0$:
if $j_1+j_2+j_3$ is odd, then

$$\begin{pmatrix}j_1&j_2&j_3\\0&0&0\end{pmatrix}=0.$$
Here $j_1+j_2+j_3=1+2+2=5$ is odd, so

$$\begin{pmatrix}1&2&2\\0&0&0\end{pmatrix}=0,$$
and therefore
$$\langle1,0;2,0\mid2,0\rangle=0.$$

In the Hahn–polynomial representation of Clebsch–Gordan coefficients (see T.H. Koornwinder, _Clebsch–Gordan coefficients for SU(2) and Hahn polynomials_, Nieuw Archief voor Wiskunde (3) 29 (1981), 140–155, and Nikiforov–Suslov–Uvarov, _Classical Orthogonal Polynomials of a Discrete Variable_, Springer, 1991), each fixed channel can be written in terms of a Hahn polynomial $Q_n(x;a,b,N)$ in the discrete variable $x=j_1-m_1$. Since $\langle1,0;2,0\mid2,0\rangle=0$ and this coefficient is proportional to $Q_n(x;a,b,N)$ evaluated at the corresponding $x=j_1-m_1$, that value of $x$ is a root of the associated Hahn polynomial.
