For large $\nu > 0$, the nonuniform Debye asymptotic formula for $J_\nu(\nu z)$ in the oscillatory region $z > 1$ is
$$J_\nu(\nu z) \sim \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi\nu}}\frac{1}{(z^2-1)^{1/4}}\cos\!\left(\nu\sqrt{z^2-1} - \nu\arccos(1/z) - \frac{\pi}{4}\right).$$
This formula holds for each fixed $z > 1$ as $\nu \to \infty$.

Claim: One may recover the correct leading-order asymptotic of $J_\nu(\nu)$ by taking the limit $z \to 1^+$ in the Debye formula above.

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion. If the claim is false, find the correct leading-order asymptotic for $J_\nu(\nu)$ as $\nu \to \infty$.
