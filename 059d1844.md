Let $P_\ell^m(x)$ denote the associated Legendre functions on $[-1,1]$ for integers $\ell \geq m \geq 0$. For fixed $x_0 \in (-1,1)$ and $m \geq 0$, consider the normalized weighted sum

$$S_N^{(m)}(x_0) = \sum_{\ell=m}^{N} \frac{(2\ell+1)(\ell-m)!}{(\ell+m)!} \cdot P_\ell^m(x_0) \cdot P_\ell^m(x_0)$$

Claim: For all $m \geq 0$ and $x_0 \in (-1,1)$, the sum satisfies the asymptotic behavior

$$S_N^{(m)}(x_0) = \frac{N^2}{2(1-x_0^2)} + O(N)$$

as $N \to \infty$.

Determine with rigorous proof whether the claim is True or False.

Conclusion False.

Already the case $m=0$ disproves the claim. Then $P_\ell^0=P_\ell$ and

$$S_N^{(0)}(x_0)=\sum_{\ell=0}^{N}(2\ell+1)P_\ell(x_0)^2=2\sum_{\ell=0}^{N}\frac{2\ell+1}{2}P_\ell(x_0)^2=2K_N(x_0,x_0),$$

where $K_N$ is the Legendre Christoffel–Darboux kernel. The diagonal Christoffel–Darboux formula gives

$$K_N(x_0,x_0)=\frac{N+1}{2}\bigl(P_{N+1}^\prime(x_0)P_N(x_0)-P_N^\prime(x_0)P_{N+1}(x_0)\bigr).$$

For fixed $x_0=\cos\theta_0\in(-1,1)$, the Hilb asymptotics imply

$$P_n(\cos\theta_0)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi n\sin\theta_0}}\cos\Bigl(\Bigl(n+\frac12\Bigr)\theta_0-\frac{\pi}{4}\Bigr)+O(n^{-3/2}),$$

$$P_n^\prime(\cos\theta_0)=\sqrt{\frac{2n}{\pi(\sin\theta_0)^3}}\sin\Bigl(\Bigl(n+\frac12\Bigr)\theta_0-\frac{\pi}{4}\Bigr)+O(n^{-1/2}).$$

Substituting into the expression for $K_N(x_0,x_0)$, the oscillatory phases cancel and one obtains

$$K_N(x_0,x_0)=\frac{N+1}{\pi\sin\theta_0}+O(1)=\frac{N+1}{\pi\sqrt{1-x_0^2}}+O(1).$$

Hence

$$S_N^{(0)}(x_0)=2K_N(x_0,x_0)=\frac{2N}{\pi\sqrt{1-x_0^2}}+O(1),\qquad N\to\infty.$$

The growth is linear in $N$, not quadratic. Since the claim fails already for $m=0$, it is false for the stated “all $m\ge0$” range.
