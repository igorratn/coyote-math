let $P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)$ be the jacobi polynomials with parameters $\alpha, \beta > -1$, orthogonal on $[-1, 1]$ with respect to the weight $(1-x)^\alpha(1+x)^\beta$. let

$$d_n^2 = \int_{-1}^1 (1-x)^\alpha (1+x)^\beta \bigl(P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)\bigr)^2 \, dx$$

denote the $L^2$-normalization constant.

determine, with rigorous proof, whether for any fixed $\epsilon > 0$ there exists a constant $C_\epsilon > 0$ such that for all $n \ge 1$,

$$\sup_{x \in [-1+\epsilon, 1-\epsilon]} \left| \frac{(1-x^2) P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n} \right| \le C_\epsilon.$$


the statement is true.

fix $\epsilon > 0$ and set $I_\epsilon = [-1 + \epsilon, 1 - \epsilon]$. since $1 - x^2$ is continuous and strictly positive on $I_\epsilon$, there exists a constant $M_\epsilon > 0$ such that

$$0 < 1 - x^2 \le M_\epsilon \quad \text{for all } x \in I_\epsilon.$$

by nikiforov–uvarov, special functions of mathematical physics, chapter ii, section 7 (jacobi polynomials), the interior estimate given there (inequality (19a) in that section) implies that for any interior interval $I_\epsilon$ there exists a constant $K_{\epsilon,\alpha,\beta} > 0$, independent of $n$ and $x$, such that for all $n \ge 1$ and all $x \in I_\epsilon$,

$$|P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)| \le K_{\epsilon,\alpha,\beta} n^{-1/2}.$$

multiplying by $1 - x^2$ and using $1 - x^2 \le M_\epsilon$ gives

$$\sup_{x \in I_\epsilon} |(1 - x^2) P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)| \le M_\epsilon K_{\epsilon,\alpha,\beta} n^{-1/2}.$$

the $L^2$ norm is

$$d_n^2 = \int_{-1}^1 (1 - x)^\alpha (1 + x)^\beta \bigl(P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)\bigr)^2 \, dx.$$

nikiforov–uvarov (same chapter and section) give the exact norm formula for jacobi polynomials in terms of gamma functions. applying stirling’s formula to that expression yields the asymptotic

$$d_n^2 \sim c^2 n^{-1} \quad (n \to \infty),$$

with $c > 0$ depending only on $\alpha, \beta$. hence there exist constants $m > 0$ and $N$ such that

$$d_n \ge m n^{-1/2} \quad \text{for all } n \ge N.$$

for $n \ge N$, combining the previous steps gives

$$\sup_{x \in I_\epsilon} \left| \frac{(1 - x^2) P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n} \right| \le \frac{M_\epsilon K_{\epsilon,\alpha,\beta} n^{-1/2}}{m n^{-1/2}} = \frac{M_\epsilon K_{\epsilon,\alpha,\beta}}{m}.$$
**
for the finitely many $1 \le n < N$, the quantity

$$\sup_{x \in I_\epsilon} \left| \frac{(1 - x^2) P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n} \right|$$

is finite, so enlarging the constant if necessary yields a single $C_\epsilon$ valid for all $n \ge 1$.

therefore, for any fixed $\epsilon > 0$ there exists $C_\epsilon > 0$ such that for all $n \ge 1$,

$$\sup_{x \in [-1 + \epsilon, 1 - \epsilon]} \left| \frac{(1 - x^2) P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n} \right| \le C_\epsilon.$$
