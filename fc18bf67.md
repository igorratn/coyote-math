Let $P_\ell^m(x)$ denote the associated Legendre functions on $[-1,1]$ with the standard normalization, where $\ell \geq 0$ and $0 \leq m \leq \ell$. These satisfy the orthogonality relation

$$\int_{-1}^1 P_\ell^m(x) P_{\ell'}^m(x) \, dx = \frac{2}{2\ell+1} \frac{(\ell+m)!}{(\ell-m)!} \, \delta_{\ell \ell'}.$$

For fixed $m \geq 1$, define the weighted cross-degree integral

$$I(\ell, m) = \int_{-1}^1 (1-x^2)^{1/2} \, P_\ell^m(x) \, P_{\ell+1}^m(x) \, dx$$

for $\ell \geq m$. The factor $(1-x^2)^{1/2}$ arises naturally in spherical geometry as the azimuthal metric factor $\sin\theta$.

Since $(1-x^2)^{1/2}$ is not a polynomial, the product $P_\ell^m(x) P_{\ell+1}^m(x)$ cannot be directly reduced using standard Legendre orthogonality, so $I(\ell, m)$ must be evaluated by other means.

Claim: For $m = 1$ and all $\ell \geq 2$, the integral $I(\ell, 1)$ is strictly positive.

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.


The claim is False. In fact, $I(\ell, 1) = 0$ for all $\ell \geq 1$.

The associated Legendre functions satisfy the parity relation $P_\ell^m(-x) = (-1)^{\ell+m} P_\ell^m(x)$. For $m = 1$, this gives $P_\ell^1(-x) = (-1)^{\ell+1} P_\ell^1(x)$ and $P_{\ell+1}^1(-x) = (-1)^{\ell+2} P_{\ell+1}^1(x)$.

The integrand is $f(x) = (1-x^2)^{1/2} P_\ell^1(x) P_{\ell+1}^1(x)$. Evaluating at $-x$:

$$f(-x) = (1-x^2)^{1/2} \cdot (-1)^{\ell+1} P_\ell^1(x) \cdot (-1)^{\ell+2} P_{\ell+1}^1(x) = (-1)^{2\ell+3} f(x) = -f(x).$$

Since $f$ is odd and the interval $[-1,1]$ is symmetric, $I(\ell, 1) = \int_{-1}^1 f(x)\,dx = 0$. $\blacksquare$
