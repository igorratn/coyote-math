In a one-dimensional superfluid ring with a periodic array of weak links and no external drive, the superfluid phase $\Theta(x,t)$ satisfies the damped sineâ€“Gordon equation $\Theta_{tt}+\zeta\Theta_t-c^2\Theta_{xx}+\sin\Theta=0$ for $x\in\mathbb{R}$, where $\zeta>0$ represents damping and $c>0$ is the sound speed. Assume zero topological (winding) number, so $\Theta(+\infty,t)=\Theta(-\infty,t)\in2\pi\mathbb{Z}$ for all $t$. The system is prepared at rest with a uniform phase offset $\Theta_t(x,0)=0$, $\Theta(x,0)=\pi+\varepsilon$, where $\varepsilon>0$ is small and spatially uniform. Determine whether $\lim_{t\to+\infty}\Theta(x,t)=0$ holds, or whether the phase relaxes to a different asymptotic state.

Reduce to the uniform ODE: spatially uniform data keep $\Theta(x,t)=\vartheta(t)$, so $\vartheta_{tt}+\zeta\vartheta_t+\sin\vartheta=0$ with $\vartheta(0)=\pi+\varepsilon$ and $\dot\vartheta(0)=0$. The energy $E(t)=\tfrac12\dot\vartheta^{2}+1-\cos\vartheta$ satisfies $\dot E=-\zeta\dot\vartheta^{2}\le0$, hence $E$ is nonincreasing. Initial acceleration is $\ddot\vartheta(0)=-\sin(\pi+\varepsilon)=\sin\varepsilon>0$, so $\vartheta$ moves upward from $\pi+\varepsilon$ toward larger angles. The initial energy is $E(0)=1-\cos(\pi+\varepsilon)=1+\cos\varepsilon<2=1-\cos\pi$, so the trajectory does not have enough energy to cross back over the saddle at $\vartheta=\pi$ toward $0$, and with damping it cannot gain energy later. Therefore the motion approaches the nearest stable minimum above, $\vartheta=2\pi$, and the full field converges uniformly $\lim_{t\to+\infty}\Theta(x,t)=2\pi$.
