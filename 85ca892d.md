Let $\Theta_{\ell m}(x)$ denote the normalized associated Legendre functions on $x\in[-1,1]$ for integers $\ell\ge m\ge0$, defined by

$$\Theta_{\ell m}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{2}\frac{(\ell-m)!}{(\ell+m)!}}\,P_\ell^m(x),$$

where $P_\ell^m(x)$ are the standard associated Legendre functions. For fixed $m\ge0$ and any $x_0\in(-1,1)$, define the diagonal kernel value

$$K_N^{(m)}(x_0,x_0)=\sum_{\ell=m}^N\Theta_{\ell m}^2(x_0).$$

Claim

There exists a constant $C(x_0,m)>0$ such that

$$\liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{K_N^{(m)}(x_0,x_0)}{N}\ge C(x_0,m).$$

Determine, with rigorous proof, whether this claim is True or False.

Conclusion True.

For integers $\ell \ge m \ge 0$ one has the identity

$$P_\ell^m(x) = (-1)^m 2^{-m} (1-x^2)^{m/2} \frac{(\ell+m)!}{\ell!} P_{\ell-m}^{(m, m)}(x)$$

hence

$$\Theta_{\ell m}(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{2}} \frac{\sqrt{(\ell-m)!}}{\ell!} \frac{(\ell+m)!}{2^m \sqrt{(\ell+m)!}} (1-x^2)^{m/2} P_{\ell-m}^{(m, m)}(x)$$

Nikiforov–Uvarov, Special Functions of Mathematical Physics, Birkhäuser, 1988, Chapter II, §7, Equation (19) gives for Jacobi polynomials the uniform bound

$$(1-x)^{\alpha/2+1/4}(1+x)^{\beta/2+1/4} |P_n^{(\alpha, \beta)}(x)| \le A, \quad -1 \le x \le 1$$

with $A$ independent of $n$. Taking $\alpha = \beta = m$, $n = \ell - m$, and fixing $x_0 \in (-1, 1)$ yields $|P_{\ell-m}^{(m, m)}(x_0)| \le C(x_0, m)$ uniformly in $\ell$. This implies the coarse bound $\Theta_{\ell m}(x_0) = O(\sqrt{\ell})$.

Now write $x_0 = \cos \theta_0$ with $\theta_0 \in (0, \pi)$. By Szegő, Orthogonal Polynomials, Chapter VIII, Theorem 8.21.8, formula (8.21.10), p. 196, the Jacobi interior oscillatory asymptotic gives, for fixed $m$ and fixed $\theta_0 \in (0, \pi)$,

$$P_{\ell-m}^{(m, m)}(\cos \theta_0) = (\ell-m)^{-1/2} k(\theta_0) \cos((\ell + \tfrac{1}{2})\theta_0 + \gamma) + O(\ell^{-3/2})$$

with explicit $k(\theta_0)$ and phase shift $\gamma$ depending only on $m$ and $\theta_0$. Using Stirling’s formula with fixed $m$ shows

$$\sqrt{\frac{2\ell+1}{2}} \frac{\sqrt{(\ell-m)!}}{\ell!} \frac{(\ell+m)!}{2^m \sqrt{(\ell+m)!}} = c_m \sqrt{\ell} \, (1 + O(\ell^{-1}))$$

for a constant $c_m > 0$. Substituting the Szegő asymptotic into the representation of $\Theta_{\ell m}$ and absorbing constants into the phase yields the sharp interior asymptotic

$$\Theta_{\ell m}(x_0)^2 = \frac{2}{\pi \sin \theta_0} \cos^2(\ell \theta_0 + \varphi) + o(1) = \frac{2}{\pi \sqrt{1-x_0^2}} \cos^2(\ell \theta_0 + \varphi) + o(1)$$

for some fixed $\varphi$. In particular $\Theta_{\ell m}(x_0) = O(1)$.

Average over $\ell = m, \dots, N$ and use $\cos^2 t = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \cos(2t)$:

$$\frac{1}{N} \sum_{\ell=m}^N \cos^2(\ell \theta_0 + \varphi) = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2N} \sum_{\ell = m}^N \cos(2\ell \theta_0 + 2\varphi)$$

The last sum is the real part of $\sum_{\ell=m}^N e^{i2\ell \theta_0}$, a geometric series with ratio $e^{i2\theta_0} \ne 1$, hence its partial sums are uniformly bounded, so the term divided by $N$ tends to $0$. Therefore

$$\frac{1}{N} \sum_{\ell=m}^N \cos^2(\ell \theta_0 + \varphi) \to \frac{1}{2}$$

and consequently

$$\frac{K_N^{(m)}(x_0, x_0)}{N} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{\ell=m}^N \Theta_{\ell m}^2(x_0) \to \frac{1}{\pi \sqrt{1-x_0^2}}$$

In particular,

$$\liminf_{N\to\infty} \frac{K_N^{(m)}(x_0, x_0)}{N} = \frac{1}{\pi \sqrt{1-x_0^2}} \ge \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{1-x_0^2}}$$

so the claim is true with $C(x_0, m) = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{1-x_0^2}}$.
