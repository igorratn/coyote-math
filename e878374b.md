For $\nu > 0$ and $x > 0$, let $I_\nu(x)$ and $K_\nu(x)$ denote the modified Bessel functions of the first and second kind, satisfying

$$x^2 u'' + x u' - (x^2 + \nu^2)u = 0.$$

Claim: For every $\nu > 0$ and every $x > 0$,

$$I_\nu(x)\,K_\nu(x) \;\leq\; \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\nu^2 + x^2}}.$$

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.

The claim is false.

Take $\nu=\frac{1}{2}$. Using the exact formulas

$I_{1/2}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi x}}\sinh x$

and

$K_{1/2}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2x}}e^{-x}$,

we get

$$I_{1/2}(x)K_{1/2}(x)=\frac{\sinh x \cdot e^{-x}}{x}=\frac{1-e^{-2x}}{2x}.$$

So the claimed inequality becomes

$$\frac{1-e^{-2x}}{2x}\le\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x^2+\tfrac{1}{4}}}.$$

Now choose $x=2$. Then

$$I_{1/2}(2)K_{1/2}(2)=\frac{1-e^{-4}}{4}\approx0.2454,$$

while

$$\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2^2+\tfrac{1}{4}}}=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{4.25}}\approx0.2425.$$

Hence

$$I_{1/2}(2)K_{1/2}(2)>\frac{1}{2\sqrt{\nu^2+x^2}},$$

so the inequality fails.

Therefore the claim is false.
