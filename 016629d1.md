Let $J_\nu(z)$ denote the Bessel function of the first kind, defined for $z>0$ by

$$J_\nu(z)=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{k!\Gamma(k+\nu+1)}\left(\frac{z}{2}\right)^{2k+\nu},$$

and let $Y_\nu(z)$ denote the Bessel function of the second kind, defined for non-integer $\nu$ by

$$Y_\nu(z)=\frac{J_\nu(z)\cos(\nu\pi)-J_{-\nu}(z)}{\sin(\nu\pi)},$$

extended to each integer $\nu=n$ by the continuous limit $Y_n(z)=\lim_{\nu\to n}Y_\nu(z)$.

Claim. For each $n\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge0}$ and every $z>0$,

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial\nu}J_\nu(z)\bigg|_{\nu=n}=\frac{\pi}{2}Y_n(z).$$

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.

The claim is false.

Proof. It suffices to disprove the claim for $n=1$.

From the defining series, for $\nu$ near $1$ and $z\to0^+$,

$$J_\nu(z)=\frac{(z/2)^\nu}{\Gamma(\nu+1)}+O(z^{\nu+2}).$$

Differentiating with respect to $\nu$ gives:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial\nu}J_\nu(z)=\frac{(z/2)^\nu}{\Gamma(\nu+1)}\left(\log\frac z2-\psi(\nu+1)\right)+O(z^{\nu+2}\log z),$$

where $\psi=\Gamma^\prime/\Gamma$ is the digamma function. Setting $\nu=1$ and using $\psi(2)=1-\gamma$, we obtain:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial\nu}J_\nu(z)\bigg|_{\nu=1} = \frac z2\left(\log\frac z2+\gamma-1\right)+O(z^3\log z).$$

In particular:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial\nu}J_\nu(z)\bigg|_{\nu=1}\to0\qquad(z\to0^+).$$

On the other hand, the standard small-$z$ expansion of $Y_1$ is:

$$Y_1(z)=-\frac{2}{\pi z}+O(z\log z)\qquad(z\to0^+),$$

hence

$$\frac{\pi}{2}Y_1(z)=-\frac1z+O(z\log z),$$

which does not tend to $0$ and in fact diverges to $-\infty$ as $z\to0^+$.

Therefore, for sufficiently small $z>0$:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial\nu}J_\nu(z)\bigg|_{\nu=1}\neq \frac{\pi}{2}Y_1(z).$$

So the claimed identity fails already at $n=1$. Hence the claim is false.
