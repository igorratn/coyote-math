The Legendre polynomials $P_n(x)$ have the generating function:

$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 2tx + t^2}} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} P_n(x) t^n$$

for $|t| < 1$ and $x \in [-1,1]$.

For fixed $x_0 \in (0,1)$ and $n \geq 1$, consider the weighted sum:

$$S_N(x_0) = \sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{P_n(x_0)}{n!}$$

Claim: For all $x_0 \in (0,1)$, the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{P_n(x_0)}{n!}$ converges, and the sum satisfies:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{P_n(x_0)}{n!} = e^{x_0} - 1$$

Determine with rigorous proof whether the claim is True or False.

The claim is False.

Since Legendre polynomials satisfy $|P_n(x)| \leq 1$ for all $n \geq 0$ and all $x \in [-1,1]$, the series converges absolutely by comparison with $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n!} = e - 1$.

To disprove the claimed identity, evaluate the sum at $x_0 = \frac{1}{10} \in (0,1)$. The first two Legendre polynomials are $P_1(x) = x$ and $P_2(x) = \frac{1}{2}(3x^2 - 1)$. At $x_0 = \frac{1}{10}$:

$$P_1\left(\frac{1}{10}\right) = \frac{1}{10}, \qquad P_2\left(\frac{1}{10}\right) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{3}{100} - 1\right) = -\frac{97}{200}.$$

The partial sum through $n=2$ is:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{2}\frac{P_n(1/10)}{n!} = \frac{1}{10} + \frac{-97/200}{2} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{97}{400} = -\frac{57}{400}.$$

The remaining tail of the series satisfies:

$$\left|\sum_{n=3}^{\infty}\frac{P_n(1/10)}{n!}\right| \le \sum_{n=3}^{\infty}\frac{|P_n(1/10)|}{n!} \le \sum_{n=3}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n!} = e - \frac{5}{2}.$$

Hence, the full sum satisfies:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{P_n(1/10)}{n!} \le -\frac{57}{400} + \left(e - \frac{5}{2}\right) = e - \frac{1057}{400}.$$

The full sum is thus bounded above by $e - \frac{1057}{400}$. 
Since $e - \frac{1057}{400} < e^{1/10} - 1$ numerically, the equality fails at $x_0 = \frac{1}{10}$.
