Let$$I_n = n^2 \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x^{n}}{1+2x+\frac{2}{n}}\,dx.$$Compute$$L = \lim_{n\to\infty}\Big( I_n - \frac{n}{3}\Big).$$Split the integrand at the value of the denominator at $x=1$:$$\frac{1}{1+2x+\frac{2}{n}} = \frac{1}{3+\frac{2}{n}} + \left(\frac{1}{1+2x+\frac{2}{n}}-\frac{1}{3+\frac{2}{n}}\right).$$Evaluate the constant piece exactly:$$n^2 \int_0^1 \frac{x^n}{3+\frac{2}{n}}\,dx = \frac{n^2}{3+\frac{2}{n}} \cdot \frac{1}{n+1} = \frac{n}{3} - \frac{2}{9} + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).$$Linearize the remainder around $x=1$:$$\frac{1}{1+2x+\frac{2}{n}}-\frac{1}{3+\frac{2}{n}} = -\frac{(1+2x+\frac{2}{n})-(3+\frac{2}{n})}{(1+2x+\frac{2}{n})(3+\frac{2}{n})} = -\frac{2(x-1)}{(3+\frac{2}{n})^2} + O((x-1)^2).$$Use the identity $(x-1)x^n = x^{n+1}-x^n$ to get an exact moment:$$n^2 \int_0^1 x^n(x-1)\,dx = n^2 \left(\frac{1}{n+2}-\frac{1}{n+1}\right) = \frac{n^2}{(n+1)(n+2)} = 1 + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).$$Assemble the remainder contribution:$$n^2 \int_0^1 x^n \left(\frac{1}{1+2x+\frac{2}{n}}-\frac{1}{3+\frac{2}{n}}\right) dx = -\frac{2}{(3+\frac{2}{n})^2} \cdot [1 + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)] = -\frac{2}{9} + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).$$Add the two pieces:$$I_n = \left(\frac{n}{3}-\frac{2}{9}\right) + \left(-\frac{2}{9}\right) + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right) = \frac{n}{3} - \frac{4}{9} + O\left(\frac{1}{n}\right).$$Take the limit:$$L = \lim_{n\to\infty}\left(I_n - \frac{n}{3}\right) = -\frac{4}{9}.$$
