## STEP 1 — Prompt

On a surface of constant positive Gaussian curvature $K = 2$, a geodesic circle of radius $r = \tfrac{1}{3}$ is traversed counterclockwise. A tangent vector is parallel-transported once completely around the circle.

## STEP 5 — Failure Rationale Summary

The dominant failure across all responses is the sign convention in the holonomy formula. The correct formula for a counterclockwise loop enclosing area $A$ on a surface of constant positive Gaussian curvature $K$ is

$$\Delta\theta = -K \cdot A.$$

Models consistently applied $+K \cdot A$, obtaining $+0.685$ instead of $-0.685$. This is not a rounding error or arithmetic slip — it reflects a genuine conceptual misunderstanding of the orientation convention in the Gauss-Bonnet theorem: a counterclockwise traversal on a positively curved surface causes the parallel-transported vector to rotate clockwise (negatively) relative to the initial direction.

## STEP 6 — Step-by-Step Solution + Final Answer

Step 1: State the holonomy formula

For a simple closed loop traversed counterclockwise on a surface of constant positive Gaussian curvature $K$, the signed rotation angle acquired by a parallel-transported vector is

$$\Delta\theta = -K \cdot A,$$

where $A$ is the area enclosed by the loop. The negative sign encodes the orientation convention: counterclockwise traversal on a positively curved surface produces a clockwise (negative) rotation of the transported vector. This follows from the Gauss-Bonnet theorem applied to the disk bounded by the loop.

Step 2: Compute the area of the geodesic disk

On a surface of constant curvature $K > 0$, the area enclosed by a geodesic circle of radius $r$ is not $\pi r^2$ but rather

$$A = \frac{2\pi}{K}\!\left(1 - \cos\!\left(\sqrt{K}\,r\right)\right).$$

Substituting $K = 2$ and $r = \tfrac{1}{3}$:

$$A = \frac{2\pi}{2}\!\left(1 - \cos\!\left(\sqrt{2} \cdot \tfrac{1}{3}\right)\right) = \pi\!\left(1 - \cos\!\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\right).$$

Evaluating numerically with five significant figures: $\sqrt{2}/3 \approx 0.47140\ \text{rad}$, $\cos(0.47140) \approx 0.89094$, so

$$A \approx \pi \cdot 0.10906 \approx 0.34271\ \text{rad}^2.$$

Step 3: Apply the holonomy formula

$$\Delta\theta = -K \cdot A = -2 \cdot 0.34271 \approx -0.68542\ \text{rad}.$$

Rounded to three significant figures:

$$\Delta\theta \approx -0.685\ \text{rad}.$$

Step 4: Confirm sign by geometric reasoning

The Gauss-Bonnet theorem states that for a smooth simple closed curve $\partial\Sigma$ bounding a region $\Sigma$,

$$\oint_{\partial\Sigma} \kappa_g\, ds + \iint_\Sigma K\, dA = 2\pi,$$

where $\kappa_g$ is the geodesic curvature. The deficit angle — the amount by which the holonomy rotation deviates from $2\pi$ — is $\iint_\Sigma K\, dA = K \cdot A > 0$. For a counterclockwise loop, the deficit is subtracted from $2\pi$, so the actual rotation is $2\pi - KA$. Measuring relative to the identity (i.e., subtracting $2\pi$), the net rotation is $-KA$, confirming the sign.

**Final Answer:** $-0.685$

**Final Answer Format:** $-0.685$
