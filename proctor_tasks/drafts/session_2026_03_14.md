### 3. Key Insight: What Doesn't Work
- **Any exactly solvable potential** in the NU/special functions literature → models know it
- **Explicit BCs** (Robin, spring, impedance) → models handle correctly
- **Named framework cues** (spin-0 → KG, sech² → Pöschl-Teller) → models pattern-match correctly
- **Standard NU reductions** → models have seen all the arXiv papers

### 4. NU Paper Analysis (arXiv:2411.00804)
- Gordillo-Núñez (Oct 2024) comprehensive review of NU method
- Key finding: Corollary 3.1 (standard bound state completeness) **doesn't always apply** (e.g., Morse potential)
- The paper emphasizes careful justification of when polynomial solutions are valid — most papers skip this
- Proctor opportunity: problems where the standard NU recipe gives eigenvalues that LOOK correct but aren't properly justified
