A spinless particle of rest mass $m = 300.0$ MeV/$c^2$ is described by the Klein-Gordon equation with equal Lorentz scalar and vector potentials, $S(r) = V(r)$, where

$$V(r) = -V_0\,(\hbar c)\alpha\,\frac{e^{-\alpha r}}{1 - e^{-\alpha r}}$$

with dimensionless depth $V_0 = 2.0$ and range parameter $\alpha = 0.500$ fm$^{-1}$. Use $\hbar c = 197.327$ MeV$\cdot$fm.

Under equal scalar-vector coupling, the radial Klein-Gordon equation for the $s$-wave ($\ell = 0$) reduces to an effective Schrödinger-like equation with an energy-dependent effective potential.

For the ground state ($n_r = 0$, no radial nodes), determine the particle-branch ($E > 0$) binding energy $E_b = mc^2 - E$ in MeV. Report your answer rounded to three significant figures.

---
