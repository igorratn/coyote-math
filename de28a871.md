Consider the physicistsâ€™ Hermite polynomials $H_n(x)$ on $(-\infty,\infty)$ with $n\ge1$. For $z\in\mathbb C\setminus\mathbb R$, define

$$Q_n(z)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{e^{-t^2}H_n(t)}{z-t}dt.$$

Claim. For every $n\ge1$,

$$\lim\limits_{|z|\to\infty,\ z\in\mathbb C\setminus\mathbb R}\frac{zQ_n(z)}{Q_{n-1}(z)}=n,$$

and the limit holds uniformly for $z$ in any closed sector bounded away from the real axis. That is, for each fixed $\delta\in(0,\pi/2)$, the convergence as $|z|\to\infty$ is uniform for $0<\delta\le|\arg z|\le\pi-\delta$.

Determine, with rigorous justification, whether the claim is true or false.

The claim is True.

To determine the behavior of $Q_n(z)$ as $|z|\to\infty$, we use the following exact algebraic identity. For any integer $N\ge0$ and any $z\ne t$,

$$\frac{1}{z-t} = \sum_{k=0}^{N}\frac{t^{k}}{z^{k+1}} + \frac{t^{N+1}}{z^{N+1}}\frac{1}{z-t}.$$

This identity holds for all real $t$ and all complex $z\notin\mathbb R$. Taking $N=n$ and substituting into the definition of $Q_n(z)$ gives:

$$Q_n(z)=\sum_{k=0}^{n}\frac{1}{z^{k+1}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{-t^2}H_n(t)t^{k}\,dt +\frac{1}{z^{n+1}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{e^{-t^2}H_n(t)t^{n+1}}{z-t}\,dt.$$

Since the sum is finite, the integrals may be taken term by term. The last term is a remainder $R_n(z)$ satisfying $R_n(z)=O(|z|^{-n-2})$ as $|z|\to\infty$, uniformly for $0<\delta\le|\arg z|\le\pi-\delta$.

By the structural property of orthogonality, the integral $\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-t^2} H_n(t) t^k dt$ vanishes for all $k < n$. The first non-vanishing term in the series occurs precisely when $k = n$. Therefore, the leading-order asymptotic behavior of $Q_n(z)$ is:

$$Q_n(z) \sim \frac{1}{z^{n+1}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-t^2} H_n(t) t^n dt$$

For the physicists' Hermite polynomials, the value of this integral is $\sqrt{\pi} n!$. Thus, the leading term is:

$$Q_n(z) \sim \frac{\sqrt{\pi} n!}{z^{n+1}}$$

Applying this result to the ratio in the claim:

$$\lim\limits_{|z|\to\infty,\ 0<\delta\le|\arg z|\le\pi-\delta}\frac{zQ_{n}(z)}{Q_{n-1}(z)} = \lim\limits_{|z|\to\infty}\frac{z\cdot\frac{\sqrt\pi\,n!}{z^{n+1}}}{\frac{\sqrt\pi\,(n-1)!}{z^{n}}} = \frac{n!}{(n-1)!}=n.$$

Conclusion: True.
