For $n \geq 1$ and $z > 0$, define

$$F_n(z) = \frac{1}{n^2} \int_0^{nz} J_n(u)\, du,$$

where $J_n$ denotes the Bessel function of the first kind of integer order $n$. The integral $\int_0^w J_n(u)\, du$ is well-defined for all $w > 0$ and $n \geq 1$.

For $0 < a < 1$, define the Kapteyn-Bessel antiderivative sum

$$\mathcal{K}(a) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} F_n(a).$$

This series converges absolutely for $0 < a < 1$ since $|F_n(a)| = O(r^n)$ for some $r < 1$ depending on $a$.

Claim: For all $0 < a < 1$,

$$\mathcal{K}(a) = \frac{1}{2}\bigl[(1-a)\ln(1-a) + a\bigr].$$

Determine whether this claim is True or False, and give a rigorous proof of your conclusion.



The claim is False.

By substitution $u = nt$ in each $F_n$, we get $F_n(a) = \frac{1}{n}\int_0^a J_n(nt)\,dt$. Since $|J_n(nt)/n| = O(r^n)$ for $0 \leq t \leq a < 1$, the series $\sum J_n(nt)/n$ converges absolutely and uniformly on $[0, a]$, justifying termwise integration. Thus $\mathcal{K}(a) = \int_0^a S(t)\,dt$ where $S(t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{J_n(nt)}{n}$. The claim is equivalent to $S(t) = -\frac{1}{2}\ln(1-t)$ for $0 < t < 1$. We disprove this by comparing Taylor coefficients.

Using $J_n(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^k}{k!(n+k)!}(z/2)^{n+2k}$ and noting $\frac{J_n(nt)}{n} = O(t^n)$, only $n = 1$ and $n = 3$ contribute to the $t^3$ coefficient of $S(t)$:

- $n = 1$: $J_1(t) = t/2 - t^3/16 + O(t^5)$. Coefficient of $t^3$: $-1/16$.
- $n = 3$: $J_3(3t)/3 = (3t)^3/(48 \cdot 3) + O(t^5) = 3t^3/16 + O(t^5)$. Coefficient of $t^3$: $3/16$.
- $n = 2$: $J_2(2t)/2 = t^2/4 + O(t^4)$. No $t^3$ term.
- $n \geq 4$: $O(t^n) = O(t^4)$. No $t^3$ term.

Total $t^3$ coefficient of $S(t)$: $-1/16 + 3/16 = 1/8$.

But $-\frac{1}{2}\ln(1-t) = t/2 + t^2/4 + t^3/6 + \cdots$ has $t^3$ coefficient $1/6$.

Since $1/8 \neq 1/6$, $S(t) \neq -\frac{1}{2}\ln(1-t)$, and therefore $\mathcal{K}(a) \neq \frac{1}{2}[(1-a)\ln(1-a) + a]$. Explicitly, $\mathcal{K}(a) = a^2/4 + a^3/12 + a^4/32 + O(a^5)$ while the claim gives $a^2/4 + a^3/12 + a^4/24 + O(a^5)$. The $a^4$ coefficients differ: $1/32 \neq 1/24$. $\blacksquare$
