Let $P_\ell^m(z)$ denote the Ferrers associated Legendre functions ($\ell \ge m \ge 0$), analytically continued from $(-1,1)$ to the slit plane  
$\mathbb{C} \setminus ((-\infty,-1] \cup [1,\infty))$ using the standard real-axis normalization.

Fix a compact set $K \subset (-1,1)$ and integers $\ell \ge m \ge 0$.  
Assume $x \in K$ satisfies $P_\ell^m(x) \ne 0$, and define for $y > 0$ small  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,y) = \arg\!\left( \frac{P_\ell^m(x+iy)}{P_\ell^m(x-iy)} \right),
$$  
where the argument is chosen continuously for $y > 0$ and  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,0^+) = 0.
$$

Claim: As $y \to 0^+$,  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,y) = 2y \,\partial_x \log |P_\ell^m(x)| + O(y^3),
$$  
where the $O(y^3)$ remainder is uniform for $x$ ranging in any compact subset $K_0 \subset K$ on which  
$$
|P_\ell^m(x)| \ge c > 0 \quad \text{(with $c$ fixed)}.
$$

Determine, with proof, whether the claim is true.

The claim is True

Let $f(z) = P_\ell^m(z)$. Fix $\ell, m$ and a compact set $K_0 \subset K$ such that  
$$
|f(x)| \ge c > 0 \quad \text{on } K_0.
$$

Since $f$ is analytic on $\mathbb{C} \setminus ((-\infty,-1] \cup [1,\infty))$ and $K_0 \subset (-1,1)$, there exists a neighborhood $U$ of $K_0$ contained in the slit plane on which $f$ has no zeros. Hence an analytic branch of $\log f$ exists on $U$ and  
$$
(\log f)' = \frac{f'}{f}.
$$

For $x \in K_0$ and $y > 0$ small, with the argument chosen continuously and  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,0^+) = 0,
$$  
we have  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,y) = \operatorname{Im}\bigl( \log f(x+iy) - \log f(x-iy) \bigr).
$$

By Taylor expansion of $\log f$ at the real point $x$,  
$$
\log f(x \pm iy) = \log f(x) \pm iy (\log f)'(x) - \frac{y^2}{2} (\log f)''(x) \mp \frac{iy^3}{6} (\log f)'''(x) + O(y^4),
$$  
uniformly for $x \in K_0$.

Subtracting cancels the even powers of $y$ and gives  
$$
\log f(x+iy) - \log f(x-iy) = 2iy \, (\log f)'(x) + O(y^3).
$$

Taking imaginary parts,  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,y) = 2y \, \operatorname{Re}\bigl( (\log f)'(x) \bigr) + O(y^3).
$$

For $x \in (-1,1)$ with the Ferrers normalization, $f(x)$ is real, so  
$$
(\log f)'(x) = \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)}
$$  
is real wherever $f(x) \ne 0$. Hence  
$$
\operatorname{Re}\bigl( (\log f)'(x) \bigr) = \partial_x \log |f(x)| = \partial_x \log |P_\ell^m(x)|.
$$

Thus  
$$
\Phi_\ell^m(x,y) = 2y \, \partial_x \log |P_\ell^m(x)| + O(y^3),
$$  
with the $O(y^3)$ remainder uniform for $x \in K_0$.

The claim is therefore true under the stated nonvanishing condition.
