For integers $\ell \ge m \ge 0$, let $P_\ell^m(x)$ denote the associated Legendre functions on $[-1,1]$. Fix $m \ge 0$ and $\theta \in (0,\pi)$, and set $x_\ell = \cos(\theta/\ell)$ so that $x_\ell \to 1$ as $\ell \to \infty$. Define:

$$M_\ell^m(\theta) = \ell^{-m} P_\ell^m \left( \cos \frac{\theta}{\ell} \right)$$

Claim: For all $m \ge 0$ and all $\theta \in (0, \pi)$, the limit

$$\lim_{\ell \to \infty} M_\ell^m(\theta) = \frac{\theta^m}{2^m m!}$$

exists and equals the stated expression. Determine, with rigorous proof, whether the claim is True or False.



Claim: False.

Proof. Fix $m \ge 0$ and $\theta \in (0, \pi)$. The Mehler–Heine asymptotic for associated Legendre functions, using the convention $P_\ell^m(x)=(-1)^m(1-x^2)^{m/2}\frac{d^m}{dx^m}P_\ell(x)$, gives:

$$\lim_{\ell \to \infty} \ell^{-m} P_\ell^m \left( \cos \frac{\theta}{\ell} \right) = \left( \frac{\theta}{2} \right)^m J_m(\theta)$$

where $J_m$ is the Bessel function of the first kind.

In particular, for $m = 0$, the limit is:

$$\lim_{\ell \to \infty} P_\ell \left( \cos \frac{\theta}{\ell} \right) = J_0(\theta)$$

Take $\theta = \pi/2$. The claimed limit would give 1 (since $\frac{(\pi/2)^0}{2^0 \cdot 0!} = 1$). But by the Mehler–Heine limit we get $J_0(\pi/2)$, and $J_0(\pi/2) \approx 0.472 \neq 1$. Hence the stated formula fails already for $m = 0$.

Therefore the claim is false.
