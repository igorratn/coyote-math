Let $P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)$ be the jacobi polynomials with parameters $\alpha, \beta > -1$, orthogonal on $[-1, 1]$ with respect to the weight $(1-x)^\alpha(1+x)^\beta$.

let

$$d_n^2 = \int_{-1}^1 (1-x)^\alpha(1+x)^\beta\bigl(P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)\bigr)^2 \, dx$$

denote the $L^2$-normalization constant (so $d_n \sim c n^{-1/2}$ as $n \to \infty$).

determine, with rigorous proof, whether the following statement is true or false:

for any fixed $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a constant $C_\epsilon > 0$ such that for all $n \ge 1$,

$$\sup_{x \in [-1+\epsilon, 1-\epsilon]} \left| \frac{\sqrt{n}(1-x^2)P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n} \right| \le C_\epsilon.$$

the statement is false.

fix $\epsilon > 0$ and choose $x_0 \in [-1 + \epsilon, 1 - \epsilon]$. write $x_0 = \cos\theta$ with $\theta \in [\theta_\epsilon, \pi - \theta_\epsilon]$. by szegő, _orthogonal polynomials_ (4th ed., 1975), theorem 8.21.8, the interior darboux asymptotic holds uniformly for such $\theta$:

$$P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(\cos\theta) = A(\theta)n^{-1/2}\cos((n+\kappa)\theta+\phi) + O(n^{-3/2})$$

where $A(\theta) \neq 0$ and $\kappa, \phi$ depend only on $\alpha, \beta$.

since the phase advances linearly modulo $2\pi$, there exists an infinite subsequence $n_k$ such that

$$|\cos((n_k+\kappa)\theta+\phi)| \ge 1/2.$$

choose $k$ large enough so that the remainder satisfies $|O(n_k^{-3/2})| \le \frac{1}{4}|A(\theta)|n_k^{-1/2}$. then

$$|P_{n_k}^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x_0)| \ge \frac{1}{4}|A(\theta)|n_k^{-1/2}.$$

let $d_n$ denote the $L^2$-normalization constant. from the exact norm formula (szegő, eq. (4.3.3)) one has

$$d_n^2 \sim c^2 n^{-1}, \text{ hence } d_n \sim c n^{-1/2},$$

so $1/d_{n_k} \ge c_1 n_k^{1/2}$ for large $k$. since $1 - x_0^2$ is a fixed positive constant, we obtain

$$\left|\frac{\sqrt{n_k}(1-x_0^2)P_{n_k}^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x_0)}{d_{n_k}}\right| \ge (1-x_0^2)\frac{\sqrt{n_k}}{d_{n_k}}\frac{|A(\theta)|}{4}n_k^{-1/2} = (1-x_0^2)\frac{|A(\theta)|}{4}\frac{1}{d_{n_k}} \ge C n_k^{1/2} \to \infty.$$

thus the expression is unbounded at a fixed interior point, and consequently

$$\sup_{x\in[-1+\epsilon,1-\epsilon]} \left|\frac{\sqrt{n}(1-x^2)P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)}{d_n}\right|$$

cannot be bounded by any constant $C_\epsilon$. therefore, the statement is false.
